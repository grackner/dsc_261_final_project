Topic: Style - Arts

Dance Marathon Raises Over $30,000 for Local Children'ner Hospital: A Stylish Blend of Art and Generosity on October 21st in New York City


In a stunning display of style fused with humanitarian effort at Lincoln Center on Thursday evening, hundreds gathered to watch the annual Style Dance Marathon that raised over $30,000 for pediatric cancer research. From sunrise until sundown, dancers synchronized their movements and rhythms amid flashy costumes inspired by iconic fashion eras while supporting children battling this relentless disease at Mount Sinai Hospital in Manhattan.


"We wanted to create an experience that would be as memorable for its style impact as it was effective in raising funds," said Sarah LeBlanc, one of the event's choreographers and a fashion enthusiast who has worked with several high-end brands like Gucci and Chanel. "By incorporating elements from various periods into our performances – think 1920s flappers to futuristic runway looks – we not only captivate the audience but also celebrate creativity in a unique art form."


As dancers donned sequined dresses, feather boas, and tailored suits reminiscent of bygone eras alongside modern interpretations like LED-lit bodysuits for contemporary looks onstage under the bright New York skyline lights, attendees cheered every innovative step. Amidst this spectacle stood Emily Russo from 32nd Street High School, who've participated in marathons raising funds over several years:

"I never imagined I could enjoy my passion for dance and fashion while supporting such a noble cause," she said between breaths during an energetic routine. "This event makes me feel like everyone is part of something bigger."


Charities benefit directly from these creative fundraisers, with all proceeds going towards pediatric oncology wards at the hospital where children receive not just treatment but also emotional support through arts and activities during their therapy. Dr. Mark Houghton, a leading physician in his field stated:

"Every dollar raised is pivotal to improving care quality – we are grateful for these dedicated supporters."


As the marathon came to an end at sunset after 12 grueling hours of non-stop dancing and networking, organizers announced plans to make next year's event even more grandiose. LeBlanc revealed:

"We are looking into expanding our reach across different boroughs in NYC – this is just the beginning."
