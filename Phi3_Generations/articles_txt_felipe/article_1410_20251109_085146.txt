As temperatures plummet across New England this weekend, a rare and potentially devastating ice storm is forecasted to hit by Sunday evening in Boston and surrounding areas according to the National Weather Service (NWS). The arctic blast will cause freezing rain accumulation of up to two inches, with winds gusting at 25 mph expected alongside subfreezing temperatures. As residents brace for impactful weather conditions set to arrive on Sunday night and Monday morning, officials have raised concerns over downed power lines which could lead to widespstatic outages across the region affecting thousands of homes and businesses as soon as midnight tonight [Lisa Thompson/The Boston Globe]

"We are preparing for an ice storm with significant accumulation forecasted. Residents must secure their properties immediately, stay indoors when possible, refrain from using generators inside buildings or near windows to prevent carbon monoxide poisoning," warned Mayor Joseph Patterson in a press conference on Saturday evening. The city's emergency services and utility providers are deploying extra resources for swift response times as the storm is expected to last through Tuesday with gradual snowfall predicted by Wednesday night [Jake Collins/Boston Herald].

The NWS has issued a winter weather advisory, highlighting that road conditions could become treacherous and hazardous due to black ice formation. The accumulation of heavy precipitation on already icy roads can result in accidents or traffic congestion as reported by local law enforcement departments across the city [Emily White/The Boston Globe].

"This storm is going to test our infrastructure, but we are confident that with proactive measures taken today and tomorrow, emergency services will be able to provide immediate support," assured City Councilor Sarah Roberts. Residents have been encouraged by local authorities to clear walkways near their homes ahead of time; additionally, the city is coordinating snow plows ready for deployment during freezing rain fall [Mike Reynolds/The Boston Herald].

To cope with possible power outages stemming from downed wires due to heavy accumulation and falling trees in some areas, officials advise residents to prepare emergency kits including flashlights, bottled water, non-perishable snacks along with first aid supplies. A community center near Beacon Hill is being designated as a temporary warming station for the most vulnerable populations during this crisis [Andrea Bennett/Boston Herald].

"In years past similar storm situations have resulted in power outages lasting up to 72 hours, but with proper safety measures and community cooperation I am hopeful we will be able to minimize disru0ptions," said John Harper of the city's Department of Public Safety. Local hospitals are on alert for a surge of patients requiring medical attention due to accidents or cold-related emergencies as this weather event unfolds [Natalie Young/CNN].

The impact will also be seen in local schools where officials have announced closures from Sunday morning until Wednesday afternoon. The Boston Public Schools District's administration urges residents with students who rely on school transportation to arrange alternative routes or accommodations for the duration of the storm [Laura Adams/Boston Herald].
