Topic: Travel - Destinations

A small tourist town in Bali has become a hotspot for eco-conscious travelers who are looking to immerse themselves in sustainable luxury amidst the lush landscapes of Indonesia's most visited island nation. Nestled on the pristine coastline, Canggu Village is now renowned not only as one of Bali'threeth heaven but also for its unique combination of upscale hotels and environmentally friendly practices that have been in place since 2015 under a sustainability initiative by local businesses. The move was aimed at reducing the ecological footprint on Balinese land while boosting tourism revenue without exploiting natural resources or culture, as per an interview with Bali's Environmental Minister Eko Sampurno last month.

"We believe that sustainability is not just a trend; it’s our way of life here in Canggu Village," said Dr. Fajar Tanoeja, Executive Director at the Center for Green Development and Tourism, based out of Bali's leading environmental think-tank The International Institute of Ecology Studies (IIIES). "We want to provide top-notch services while respecting our environment."

One major accomplishment in Canggu Village is its comprehensive waste management system. This includes a recycling and compost facility that turns organic wastes into fertilizers for local agriculture, creating jobs along the way. The tourism sector has been able to flourish thanks to this conscious effort as well; 30% of all hotels now boast some form of eco-luxury experience with features like solar powered energy and organic gardens on their grounds where guests can help harvest produce for local markets.

According to Balinese cultural expert, I Wayan Dwiputra from the Bali Cultural Preservation Society (BCPS), Canggu Village's approach goes beyond waste management as it also promotes engagement between tourists and locals in order to foster mutual understanding of both cultures. "I’ve seen people come here, take part in traditional Balinese dances or even just enjoy a quiet cup of coffee with local farmers discussing their day," Dwiputra said during his interview last week at the annual 'Balai Eco-Tourism' festival held by BCSPS. "It helps to maintain our unique way of life here in Canggu and teaches respect for one another’s cultural background."

Aside from sustainability efforts on a micro level, Balinese authorities have been working with international partners like the United Nations Development Programme (UNDP) since 2018 to expand environmentally friendly practices across all levels of society. Their collaboration is expected not only strengthen eco-tourism here in Bali but also inspire sustainable initiatives throughout Indonesia and Asia as a whole, according to I Komang Triwi from UNDP's regional office based out of Jakarta last month at the World Eco Tourism Summit.

"Canggu Village is setting an example that can be replicated elsewhere," he said in his keynote speech during this international event where delegates shared experiences and lessons learned on ecological conservation efforts throughout Asia Pacific regions. "It’s vital for tourists to understand the importance of their impact on local environments while enjoying luxury travel here."
