Dazzling at Davos: Global Leaders Embrace a Sustainable Luxury Dress Code in Switzerland's World Economic Forum Meeting

STOCKHOLM (CNN) - In what was expected to be a week of discussing the world economy and climate change, this year’seludden shift towards sustainability took center stage when global leaders started adopting an eco-friendly dress code at the World Economic Forum in Davos.

The influential annual gathering aimed at shaping key economic policies saw a sea of green as business tycoons, celebrities and heads of state all arrived donned with environmentally conscious fashion choices ranging from bamboo-based fabrics to recycled metals accessories on Friday.

This significant change in attire highlighted the importance climate change issues were expected to play throughout discussions at this year's meeting, which runs through January 23rd and brings together more than a thousand participants from various sectors of society including politicians, business leaders, celebrities and academics. The United Nations Development Programme (UNDP), World Economic Forum’s key partner in the annual discussions on global development challenges also sees itself as an advocate for environmentally responsible policies when it comes to fashion at Davos meetings now considered a trendsetter rather than just another high-profile event.

"I've always tried not only looking good, but doing what I can about the planet too," said Emma Watson who sported her signature Gucci dress made from organic cotton during Friday’s ceremony outside Credit Suisse Arena before entering into plenary sessions inside where she would later meet with government officials and entrepreneurs.
 
Aside from celebrities like Madonna, Leonardo DiCaprio wore custom-made suits by fashion pioneer Virgil Abloh's label Off-White as he attended this year’s event in the Swiss alpine resort town where global decision makers are expected to unite over economic growth and shared prosperity.
 
Environmental activists have also shown support for sustainable attire with celebrities often using their platform on social media such as Instagram which has been flooded by pictures of stars wearing eco-friendly fashion items during the weeklong meetings that usually attracts high pressures, and a lot more heat.
 
While some attendees expressed concerns about how difficult it may be to find sustainable clothing options while traveling worldwide given their limited time before heading home after this years forum ends on January 23rd others saw the green-dress code as an opportunity rather than a hindrance.
 
“It’s not just good for me but it's great to see so many like minded individuals who have made conscious choices," said actor Tim Curry while admiring his own selfmade Tilda Swinton inspired eco jacket he purchased online after extensive research on sustainable fashion brands. 
 
The increasingly popular trend has also garnered support from industry experts such as Dame Anita Roddick OBE, an influential philanthropist who founded The Body Shop and continues to champion ethical practices within the beauty sector; she believes this could potentially lead a change that extends beyond Davos meetings.
