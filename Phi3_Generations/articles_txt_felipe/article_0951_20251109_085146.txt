In a major cybersecurity incident at the nation's largest tech firm this past weekend, sensitive data from millions of accounts was potentially exposed to unautstatic users worldwide last Saturday according to company officials and industry analysts. The breach reportedly took place on March 23rd in California when an anonymous hacker exploited a flaw within the corporation's network security system.

"We immediately launched our emergency response protocol as soon as we became aware of unauthorized access to certain segments of our data storage," said Jane Smith, Director of Public Relations for Tech Giant Inc., in an official statement released late Sunday night. "Our priority is the security and privacy of user information."

Tech giant revealed that while financial details such as credit card numbers were not exposed during this breach due to our advanced encryption methods, personal contact lists could have been accessed along with some non-financial data like birthdates or email addresses. 

A spokesperson for the company added in an interview on Monday morning: "We are continuously working tirelessly at strengthening security and taking immediate steps to address this issue." The firm has notified all potentially affected users through personalized emails prompting them to change their passwords immediately, but it is unclear how many accounts were accessed.

The company's vulnerability was detected by the IT team on Sunday afternoon; however, reports began flooding in about unauthorized access as early as 2:15pm Pacific Time when an unusual surge of traffic started showing up from IP addresses not belonging to Tech Giant Inc.'s usual user base. Our focus is on securing our system, ensuring data safety for our users, and strengthening defenses against future threats."
