In Nairobi on Tuesday, a local government official announced plans to invest heavily in infrastructure projects across Kenya as part of an ambitious bid to revitalize the economy and create jobs amid rising poverty levels nationwide. The plan, set forth by Mayor Johnson Muthama, would see USD 3 billion allocated towards developing roads, bridges, schools, healthcare facilities, and other public amenities over a five-year period starting from April this year with an annual budget increase of 10%.

"Kenya deserves better living conditions for her citizens," Muthama said during the press conference held at City Hall. "We understand our people's pain as they face increasing poverty, joblessness and social unrest." He also stated that infrastructure development is a proven way to stimulate economic growth by attracting investment opportunities in various sectors such as real estate, tourism and manufacturing industries which would further create employment.

According to the latest government report released yesterday on poverty rates across Kenya'in regions: Northern has 45%, Eastern at 38% with Rural areas experiencing higher levels compared to urban cities due largely in part because of lacking access and investment into infrastructure for decades. "The North-Eastern provinces have been historically neglected, hence their high poverty rates," a professor from the University of Nairobi's School of Economics noted during an interview on national radio earlier today explaining how economic disparities arise between regions within countries and emphasizing that infrastructure development is essential for balanced growth.
