Topic: Style - Fashion

In a fashionable twist on sustainability and social awareness, New York'selfccentric design house Glamour by Night unveiled its latest runway showcase in Times Square last Saturday evening where models strutted down the catwalk sporting attire crafted from repurposed ocean plastic. The collection aims to draw attention not only towards innovative fashion but also environmental conservation, as designer Eliza Thornberry stated during her interview with Vogue's editor-in-chief Anna Wintour: "Fashion has the power to reflect and influence our world for better or worse."

The Glamour by Night runway event featured over 50 models donning outfits that blend haute couture elements with functional, recyclable materials sourced from local coastal cleanups. The design team worked tirelessly throughout the past year to curate these one-of-a-kind garments which they believe can help change public perception on plastic pollution and spark a conversation about its impact globally.

Environmental advocates praised Glamour by Night's initiative, seeing it as an opportunity for fashion houses across the world to follow suit in incorporating eco-friendly practices within their design processes without compromising style or quality: "This is exactly what I would expect from a brand that prides itself on innovation and leadership," said David Attenborough Fellow at Columbia University, Dr. Rebecca Lee, who attended the showcase alongside prominent fashion critics and influencers alike; as she shared her thoughts with reporters post-event: "It's heartening to see such a strong display of commitment towards sustainable practices in an industry that is notorious for its environmental footprint.
