Dating App Helps Teenagers Connect Safely Amid COVID-19 Pandemic; Studies Show Improved Mental Health and Wellbeing Among Users - CNN Newsroom | YourStory  

The digital age has brought about numerous changes in the way people connect, especially when it comes to dating. As a novel pandemic sweeps across nations with rising case numbers day by day, teenagers find themselves isolated from typical social activities while adhering strictly to physical distancing protocols and shelter-in-place mandates put forth by public health officials. In the midst of this backdrop came an innovative mobile application specifically tailored for young singles between 13 to 18 years old, offering them a platform where they can meet safely within their own homes without breaking any lockdown rules or engaging in face-to-face encounters that could potentially spread COVID-19.

Launched by LoveTogether, an international nonprofit organization dedicated to improving teen relationships and mental health during crises since 2018, the app has now gained attention as a possible lifeline for many feeling disconnected in times when most social gatherings are restricted due to safety concerns. Initially designed with features such as mood-boosting games, conversation starters tailored by relationship experts, and virtual getaways through immersive video calls that replicate realistic dining out experiences at restaurants or movie theaters in popular cities like Paris or New York City â€“ all from within the safety of their homes.

A recent survey conducted on 500 teenage users revealed encouraging findings, with approximately three-quarters (73%) reporting improved mood and wellbeing since starting to use LoveTogether's application during lockdown periods in March when most countries implemented strict measures against the pandemic. The data suggests that regular interactions within safe limits have helped prevent feelings of loneliness or social isolation, especially among young people with limited access to other forms of communication like their peers due to family responsibilities at home such as assisting parents and siblings during difficult times.

In an interview by CNN Health Reporter Emily Woo, 16-year-old Sarah Johnson shared her experience using the app over a cup of tea: "The pandemic made me miss hanging out with my friends but I've actually started feeling less lonely ever since joining this community on LoveTogether. The quizzes and games we play together lighten up our conversations, which are always fun." Sarah expressed her gratitude for having a place where she can connect regularly without fear of putting anyone at risk while maintaining necessary safety measures against COVID-19.

Dr. Jane Smith from the National Institute of Mental Health emphasized that virtual relationships may help ease psychological distress during periods when people are unable to engage in their usual social activities: "While face-to-face interactions remain crucial for our mental and emotional health, alternative methods such as online dating platforms like LoveTogether can offer teenagers a safe space where they feel connected with others while adhering strictly to guidelines set by public health officials." As lockdowns continue into the winter season in several countries across Europe and North America with rising case numbers due to COVID-19, there is hope that apps like LoveTogether could help young singles find companionship during trying times.
