In an unprecedented event that scientists are scrambling to understand, a massive heatwave swept across California on July 25th with temperatures expected to soar beyond historical highs in most areas of the state throughout August. The National Weather Service has issued its highest alert level for extreme conditions affecting millions of residents and prompted authorities to implement emergency measures such as water curfnerations, cooling centers opening across major cities like Los Angeles and San Francisco (The Associated Press).

  As temperatures continue to climb in the normally sunny state - soaring into triple digits throughout most parts on August first. Meteorologists warn that these unprecedented conditions are a direct result of climate change, with rising global average temperature trends fueling more frequent and intense heatwaves (CNN).

  Dr. Emily Hayes, head climatologist at UCLA's Department of Environmental Studies states "We have seen this pattern before when it comes to extreme weather events due to climate change in the Pacific Northwest - but never in California." She further added that these prolonged and severe conditions are likely an omen for what we could expect more frequently, with future implications.
