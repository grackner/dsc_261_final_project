Topic: Business - After-Hours

As night fell over downtown Manhattan on Thursday, Wall Street businesses and workers found themselves dealing with an unexpected blackout that lasted for nearly two hours starting at around 8pm local time. Around 9:45pm the lights flickered back to life just as employees were preparing to leave their workstations after a long day's grind, leaving them in disbelief about what had transpired between working hours that ended for most of Wall Street at around five PM.

"This was not something we have experienced on the streets before," said Michael Rossi, an electrician who works maintenance back-up services to some major financial institutions located along Broad Street and Avenue C near Wall Street's center. "We were just wrapping up our regular inspection when suddenly everything went dark." 

As many as thirty banks in Manhattan are now being investigated by the New York City Department of Buildings for potential code violations that could have caused or contributed to this unprecedented blackout, while power companies work around-the-clock attempting to find its root cause. The sudden loss of electricity began after a routine inspection had been completed at 8:15pm near the Chinatown neighborhood when two separate workers noticed that their electrical equipment was shutting down and they could no longer communicate with other employees or dispatchers via walkie-talkies, which are standard in this trade.

As a result of these developments, New York Governor Kathy Hochul issued an urgent statement yesterday afternoon instructing all city agencies to prepare for the possibility that another similar blackout could occur at any moment during after-hours operations across NYC and offering her guidance should one arise in future events. "While we continue investigations with confidence, it is our top priority as New Yorkers to ensure public safety," Hochul declared firmly through a city press release yesterday afternoon that has already been picked up by several outlets nationwide including CNN Business News Network for immediate distribution among their global audience of subscribers who may be affected in the event.
