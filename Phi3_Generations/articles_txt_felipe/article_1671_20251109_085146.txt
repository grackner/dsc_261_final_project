Topic: Health - Fitness

In a significant turn of events for fitness enthusiasts across New York City, Mayor Bill de Blasio announced this morning that all residents and visitors are now allowed to engage in outdoor exercise without wearing masks during daylight hours when social distancing can be maintained. The announcement was made at Tompkins Square Park yesterday evening after a thorough analysis of the city's declining COVID-19 cases, which dropped below 30 per day last week for the first time since March.

The policy change comes as Mayor de Blasio sees an opportunity to ease restrictions on physical activity while still prioritizing public health and safety measures against potential outbreak spikes caused by unvaccinated populations or new variants of concern, which are being closely monitored at local labs in partnership with the CDC.

"For too long have we placed barriers between ourselves as individuals who strive to stay fit for both our physical and mental well-being," said Mayor de Blasio during his brief speech yesterday evening held by a live audience inside the park's gazebo, which also served as an open gathering spot prior to lockdown measures being enforced in early 2020.

Dr. Asha Patel with NYC Health has been tasked with keeping tabs on how well New Yorkers adhere to outdoor exercise guidelines and assess if any adjustments need be made going forward as part of an extended monitoring period that will last for at least three more months, according to the press release issued by Mayor de Blasio's office this morning.

The policy change follows months of rigorous analysis by city officials working hand-in-hand with local health organizations like NYC Health who continually assess data trends and public behavior patterns to find safe ways for people to maintain fitness goals while minimizing risks associated during this global crisis brought on as a result from the COVID-19 pandemic back in March 2020.

"We all know how important it is staying active, but there comes an expense with that - whether one has access or not," said local business owner and park regular John Rivera who recently made changes to his routine after months away from exercise because he couldn't afford a gym membership while working as an essential worker during lockdowns here in NYC.
