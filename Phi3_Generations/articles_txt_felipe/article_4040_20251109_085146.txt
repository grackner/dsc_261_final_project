Two brothers in New York City have been arrested for their role in a complex embezzlement scheme targeting elderly residents across four states since last year. The federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI) announced today that Michael Thompson and David Thompson, both aged 32, had been taken into custody near Midtown Manhattan after months of undercover work led by Special Agent Sarah Miller unraveled the operation.

"These individuals exploited their positions within trusted senior care services to misappropriate funds meant for vulnerable citizens," said FBI Director Christopher Wray in a statement on Tuesday, as quoted by CNN New York bureau chief John Smithson. "Their arrest brings relief not only to the families affected but also serves as a reminder of our commitment to safeguarding those who need it most."

Investigators uncovered that Michael Thompson acted primarily in Washington D.C., while his younger brother David conducted operations from Florida, New Jersey, and California where several elderly individuals were defrauded out of their retirement savings totaling over $2 million between the states according to court documents obtained by DailyMail.com.

"I was shocked when I learned about what my brothers had been doing," said a tearful Michael Thompson during an interview with Special Agent Miller, who has since taken custody of him pending trial proceedings in December at United States District Court for the Southern District of New York where he is charged with federal offenses. "I wish they would have talked to me about their plans rather than hiding everything from everyone."
