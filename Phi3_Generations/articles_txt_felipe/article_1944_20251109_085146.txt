NASA Announces Record-Breaking New Spacecraft Design to Accelerate Commercial Space Travel  
November 25, 2040 - Washington D.CTravel has taken on a new frontier as NASA unveiled its latest breakthrough in space transportation at the Kennedy Space Center yesterday—an eco-friendly, reusable spaceship designed to revolutionize commercial space travel and open up low Earth orbit (LEO) tourism for everyday citizens.

Amid growing interest among investors looking towards lunar expeditions as a lucrative opportunity, NASA's new vessel promises affordable access with the aim of establishing sustainable human settlement on the Moon within two decades and potentially Mars in about 30 years following successful LEO operations. Called Project Horizon, this state-of-the-art spacecraft can carry up to seven passengers at a time for round trips lasting as little as four hours starting from Earth's surface through suborbital trajectories or lunar flybys without sacrificing safety measures and environmental care protocols.

"We are excited about Project Horizon, not only because it marks another giant leap forward in space technology but also for what its success will mean to millions of people across the globe," said NASA Administrator Bill Nelson during yesterday's unveiling ceremony attended by top officials from various government agencies as well as representatives from prominent private companies like SpaceX and Blue Origin. "Through this venture, we hope that everyone can experience life beyond Earth."

The spaceship design features cutting-edge materials such as aerogel insulation for thermal protection while using solar sails to propel itself within LEO or through lunar escape trajectories powered by ion engines fueled with inert gases. Project Horizon is expected to commence commercial operations sometime after 2045 upon completion of extensive safety and reliability tests involving mock-ups built on the NASA Testbed Facility at Kennedy Space Center, Florida.

"NASA's new era will see humanity explore not only our own planet but also venture into deep space as we learn how to live in harmony with all life forms," said Congressman Donovan Smith from North Carolina during a press conference following the event where NASA showcased its fully-equipped 1:50 scale prototype of Project Horizon. "These technological advances will bring significant economic growth and opportunities for entrepreneurs, job creation, tourism development as well."

Investors are optimistic about what lies ahead with the commercial space industry gaining increasing interest from venture capital firms across Silicon Valley who have already committed upwards of $3 billion in investments into startups working on various aspects ranging from manufacturing small habitats for lunar bases to building self-sustaining ecosystem models inside pressurized modules.
