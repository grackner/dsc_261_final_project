Topic: World - Asia

In a devastating earthquake that struck Tokyo on Sunday evening at 6:45 PM local time, preliminary reports indicate significant damage and potential loss of life in the metropolitan area surrounding Shibuya station. The magnitude-8 tremor was felt across several prefectures within Japan's densely populated western region as well as parts of neighboring countries like South Korea and China.


"We have confirmed multiple casualties, but we are still assessing the full extent of the damage," stated Chief Cabinet Secretary Yoshihide Mochizuki during a press briefing at Tokyo'terminal station on Monday morning following intense search-and-rescue operations that continued into Tuesday night.


Experts attribute this seismic event to tectonic shifts along the Philippine Sea Plate, notorious for frequent and sometimes violent movements beneath Japan’s islands. This region of Asia has a well-documented history with earthquakes dating back centuries as recorded in historical texts like "Nihon Shoki."


Local business owner Kenji Takahashi shared his experience: "It was the most terrifying thing I've ever felt, buildings were shaking uncontrollably. We had to evacuate instantly.” Emergency personnel worked around-the-clock overnight distributing aid and providing medical care in makeshift centers established at schools within Tokyo’s Shibuya district after initial reports of the disaster first emerged late Sunday night.


Researcher Dr. Akira Sato, a seismologist with Japan's Meteorological Agency who was studying recent shifts underneath the Pacific Plate in Kagoshima Prefecture prior to this tragedy, commented: "The frequency and intensity of these quakes may indicate an increasing activity beneath our oceans – it is essential we continue monitoring closely."


In response to the disaster, Japanese Prime Minister Fumio Kishida declared a national state of emergency in Tokyo and other affected areas. This comes as over 50 aftershocks have been reported since Sunday's quake struck at about 6:45 PM local time (2:15 AM GMT). Relief efforts continue, with international aid pledges pouring into the Japanese capital from countries across Asia and beyond in an effort to assist those affected by this traumatic event.
