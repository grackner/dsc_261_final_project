EU leaders convene in Brussels as energy crisis looms over Europe | CNN
Published: Friday, October 10, 2024; Updated at 9:35am EDT/ET
BRUSSELS — European Union (EU) officials gathered today to urgently address the worsening energy crisis gripping member states. The meeting comes after a string of record-breaking high temperatures in Spain and France have led to an unprecedented strain on power grids across Europe, forcing countries into rationing measures as fears grow over winter shortages.

The EU's Energy Council convened this morning with experts briefing officials about the current state of affairs — a situation made dire by geopolitical tensions and climate change impacts that have left several nations grappling for solutions amid freezing temperatures in November, which is typically one of Europe’s mildest months.

"The latest projections are frightening," says EU Climate Commissioner Laura Schmidt. "We must take decisive actions today to prevent a full-blown crisis come winter."

According to the European Wind Atlas (EWA), power output from offshore wind turbines in October decreased by nearly 20% compared to historical averages due to unanticipated high temperatures, compounding energy scarcity issues. At least six EU countries now face rolling blackouts or rationing measures as they struggle with limited gas supplies and strained electrical grids amid fears of winter shortages looming large over the continent's future stability.

"The heat is unusual for this time of year, but it also puts an unexpected pressure on our energy systems," says Energy Minister Hans Müller from Germany, one of Europe’s top coal producers and exporters to its neighbors. "It makes every day more challenging as we move towards the colder months."

Experts predict that if current trends continue unabated by substantial changes in energy sourcing or consumption patterns, power deficits could rise sharply over Christmas holidays and early January when demand peaks. EU officials are urging member states to implement emergency measures immediately — including increased renewable generation efforts like wind farms off the Belgian coast that have been operationalizing at higher rates in recent weeks but will not offset the increasing energy demands of an unusually warm continent overnight, experts say.

The EU’s Energy Commissioner has warned against complacency amid "seemingly positive developments" and urges leaders to continue working on long-term resilience strategies in conjunction with immediate responses like accelerating the transition towards more sustainable energy sources beyond oil, coal, and gas reliance.

“We need comprehensive measures that go hand in hand — from ramping up alternative fuels production capabilities all across Europe to implementing stringent conservation policies at an individual level,” she adds. “Europe cannot afford missteps or delays if we hope for a sustainable future.”
