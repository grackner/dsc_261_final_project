Emergency responders swiftly arrived at Central Station to assist a frightened commuter who had found herself stranded for over two hours. 

"I heard some loud bangs, then there was silence," recounts Sarah Thompson, describing her harrowing experience on Monday night's rush hour after work ended [#2]. "It felt like forever but it must have been more than an hour before anyone came to check."

Authorities quickly initiated emergency repairs and brought in additional safety protocol measures following the incident. As a result, transit officials announced that elevators throughout Central Station will now undergo twice-daily inspections [#3]. This new policy comes after numerous reports of broken equipment surfaced over recent months across several cities within the region's transportation network, bringing to light concerns about aging infrastructure.

"We heard her cries for help and rushed over," says Mark Stevenson.

A representative from Metro Transit Services emphasizes the need for public awareness regarding emergency protocols and how to report a stranded passenger safely. "We're working closely with community members, providing educational material on what to do if they find themselves in such situations," says Jane Parkson [#6].

As of now, there have been no other incidents reported at Central Station since Sarah Thompson’s elevator emergency; however, this unfortunate event serves as a reminder that infrastructure needs continual upkeep. In the interim, commuters are advised to remain vigilant and familiarize themselves with alternative transport methods during peak travel hours [#7].

Further details on safety improvements implemented at Central Station can be found in their press release issued this afternoon regarding Sarah Thompson’s incident: "Central Station remains committed to providing safe, reliable service. As a result of yesterday's event and consistent reports from our patrons about non-functioning elevators throughout the network we have taken immediate action," states Metro Transit Services Director Tom Roberts [#8].

With an extensive timeline for systematic checks set in motion by transit officials, Sarah Thompson is expected to be reunited with her family later today after several hours spent at Central Station.
