Topic: Style - Beauty

"New York Fashion Week is set to showcase a wave of innovative and diverse fashion designs this September in New York City as designers from around the world converge on one of America's most iconic style capitals. The event will take place at Madison Square Garden, featuring over 20 new collections that celebrate unique styles while embracing inclusivity with runway models representing different body types and ethnic backgrounds."

"Diversity is the keyword this season in fashion as New York'table of style-makers takes center stage. As part of their commitment to change within an industry struggling for racial equality, designers are bringing forth collections featuring all races wearing clothes that reflect personalized styles and confidence," said designer Renee Martinez during a press conference at the event last weekend.

"Among those models making headlines is 27-year-old Amara Williams who confidently walked in her own collection created by award-winning African American fashion designer, Marcus Anderson."

The runway show featured pieces inspired by traditional West African fabric patterns and incorporated them into modern streetwear. "This year's theme of 'unity through diversity', is a reflection on the multicultural society we live in today," stated Anderson as he watched his models strut down the catwalk for an eager audience comprising fashion enthusiasts, critics, celebrities and industry insiders."

"The show also served to highlight issues such as body positivity with plus-size models receiving equal spotlight alongside thin ones on some stages," said one model. "Being comfortable in your skin is essential for feeling confident when it comes time for fashion week headshots!" 

In addition, New York Fashion Week has announced plans to partner with local charities providing free makeovers and beauty advice workshops during the event's downtime periods throughout this year’s three-week run. "We want everyone who attends these shows feeling not only stylish but empowered as well," said an NFFW representative, adding that partnerships with nonprofits such as New York City's Glamour Girls and the Makeup Academy have already been established for this year’s showcase."

Designer Sarah Owens emphasized on inclusivity in her collection during a recent interview saying "I want everyone who sees my work to know that they are beautiful, regardless of their gender or racial background. It's about time we see more designers embracing this message and taking actionable steps towards making the fashion industry truly representative."

Reactions across social media have been mixed but mostly positive; some critics argue there is still work to be done in ensuring diversity beyond just aesthetics, while supporters hail it as progress for an inclusive world of style. As NYFW continues through September 24th with various events throughout the city this weekend alone saw more than $1 million spent on local vendors and charities alike supporting causes such as body positivity programs which aim to encourage young girls into learning about fashion, beauty standards while fostering confidence in themselves.

"It's inspiring how far we have come," shared one attendee reflecting after spending time engaging with models backstage who are not only talented but also actively involved with these programs which aim to break down industry barriers and promote inclusivity within fashion culture."
