Topic: Travel - Food & Drink

A recent culinary competition in Lyon brought together some of France's finest chefs to showcase their skills and flavors on November 3rd at La Cité du Goût (City of Taste) convention center, with the winner set to receive a cash prize funded by local businesses. The event featured an array of traditional French dishes prepared using locally sourced ingredinants—ranging from rustic bouillabaisse soup and coq au vin à l'oreille de veau, through artful patisseries like tarte Tatin with apple filling and quenelles in a rich sauce. 


Chef-instructor Pierre Lefevre explained the significance of this year’s event: "We wanted to celebrate Lyon as not just France's food capital but also its historical flavors that tell stories about where we come from.” He added, “It is important for us chefs and our diners alike to honor these recipes."


The competition drew a crowd of over 200 local citizens eagerly awaiting the tasting. Marie Dubois, a retired schoolteacher who attended her first gastronomic event this year, shared that "the variety and authenticity on display made me appreciate Lyon’s culinary culture even more.”


With proceeds to go towards supporting up-and-coming chefs through scholarships at the Lycee Gustave Eiffel in Lyon, organizer Jean Moreau said: “We believe nurturing fresh talent is key for keeping our city's gastronomic legacy alive and well.” The competition closed with a grand finale on November 4th.


As local chefs continue to innovate while preserving tradition, this event highlighted Lyon’s commitment to food as art—a reminder of why French cuisine remains an unparalleled cultural experience worldwide.
