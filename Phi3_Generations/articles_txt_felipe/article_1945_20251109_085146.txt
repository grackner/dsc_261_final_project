Amidst escalating tensions in Gaza and Israel's northern region of Golan Heights on August 15th, a surprise ceasefire announcement was made by the Israeli military command today at approximately 4:00 PM local time (2:00 AM GMT). The agreement suspended hostilities for seven days. In Jerusalem and Ramallah respectively, there are mixed reactions to this unexpected break from conflict which has raged on since early morning hours last Tuesday with no clear resolution in sight as of yet according to eyewitness accounts like those provided by resident Haim Ben-David who watched the military movements closely.

International observers including U.S., EU and Arab League diplomats have been actively monitoring developments closely since the start of these latest confrontations with concern centred around fear that any progress towards peace could be undermined by renewed violence just as negotiators attempt to broker permanent agreements aimed at resolution for decades-old unresolved questions surrounding Israel and Palestine's future territories.

Israeli Defense Minister Moshe Ya'alon commented on the ceasefire saying "This is not an end nor a beginning but rather, another pause in our continued efforts to establish security guarantees between us." He added that talks with Hamas are continuing behind closed doors while acknowledging it as difficult and complex discussions which may take months or even years before any tangible progress can be made.
