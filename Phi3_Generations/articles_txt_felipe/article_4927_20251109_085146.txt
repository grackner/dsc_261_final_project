Ecuadorian President Guillermo Lasso has been implicated in a bribery scandal involving multindeal oil company Chevron Corporation that could have major ramifications for the country's economy and reputation, according to investigative reports by El Comercio. The allegations surfaced last Thursday when former Ecuadorean officials came forward with evidence of Lasso accepting $13 million in bribes between 2014-2016 from Chevron executives seeking preferential treatment for the company's oil projects within state agencies. This information was leaked after extensive research by journalists and whistleblowers, leading to a surge of public outrage over perceived government corruption in Ecuador, an OPEC member nation with one of Latin America's most significant reliance on hydrocarbon revenues for economic stability.

Evidence presented includes financial records detailing payments made through shell companies and encrypted emails between former Energy Minister Gustavo Jalktoy and high-ranking Chevron officers, the latter including company representative Jorge Mena who is currently being held in custody on similar charges of bribery. While Lasso has denied all allegations and accused rival political parties of politically motivated attacks to undermine his presidency's credibility since taking office earlier this year after losing several by-elections, these revelations have not only cast doubt over the strength of Ecuadorian democracy but also raised concerns about how its oil wealth is being managed.

In an interview with El Comercio, former Energy Minister Gustavo Jalktoy confirmed conversations he had in 2015 and early-2016 regarding a proposed $7 billion Chevron deal that would have seen the company developing three new extraction sites within Ecuador's Yasuni National Park - an area of great environmental significance, containing indigenous cultures as well as rare wildlife species.
