Topic: Sports - Soccer

A soccer player from England has tested positive for COVID-19 just a day before his team' extramural match at Wembley Stadium in London on November 25th, casting doubt over the fixture which is expected to draw thousands of fans and generate significant revenue. Simon Carter, who plays as midfielder for Harrow Town FC after transferring from Reading United last month, was informed about his positive result early this morning by club officials during a scheduled meeting with select players ahead of their trip back home post-match day duties in London.

Carter revealed to reporters that he had been feeling under the weather since yesterday but attributed it only to general fatigue from returning as well after having traveled abroad earlier for personal reasons, unaware at the time about his potential exposure to COVID-19 which now raises questions around next week's fixture. "I never thought something like this could happen so soon into my new team," he stated before adding lightly with a smile as an attempt to ease tensions among fans and supporters,

"But I assure you all that the club stands by their protocol for handling such situations." Club chairman John Smith echoed Carter's sentiments in his own interview on Wednesday evening but also urged everyone involved not to panic. He added there are multiple layers of safety measures implemented at every step and a positive case should never deter fans from enjoying the sport they love, although he did admit that such situations do have financial implications for clubs already grappling with losses in recent seasons due to lackluster performances on pitches across England.
