In a move that has sent ripples through New York's bustling hospitality industry, three luxury hotels in Manhattan have been abruptly shut down by city officials overnight with immediate effect as of this Thursday evening. The closures include the prestigious Waldorf Astoria on Madison Avenue and two other high-end establishments: The Peninsula at One Park Avenue, and St. Regis New York Times Square. City Hall announced that a comprehensive inspection revealed significant code violations across all hotels involved in this decision, making it unsafe for guests to remain there as the city proceeds with plans to renovate these historic properties into mixed-use developments within two years' time.
