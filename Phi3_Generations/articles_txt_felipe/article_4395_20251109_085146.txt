A fire that broke out in a historic villa nestled within the Italian countrin Rome last night is now under control but has drawn attention to safety regulations around Europe's cultural landmarks. Firefighters battling flames at Palazzo Romano, built on ancient Roman ruins and dating back over 20 centuries, were able to extinguish the blaze after an estimated five hours of continuous effort early this morning (March 15).


"It was a challenging fire because it spread quickly through the wood-based structure," said Chief Fire Officer Alessandro Romano. "We managed to keep our casualty count at zero, which is always essential."

European Heritage Safety Board (EHSB) Director Maria Conti voiced concerns over the incident: "While we must preserve our cultural landmarks, it is vital to ensure they're equipped against fire hazards." Efforts are now underway by officials and local heritage groups across Europe to review safety protocols at historical properties.


The cause of the blaze remains under investigation with several suspect areas being potential sources for electrical or gas-related fires, as well as human error due to improper maintenance practices in unused sections of such ancient buildings. A committee will meet next week (March 20) at Palazzo Romano's site office on the issue and may push forward new fire prevention guidelines for historic sites across Europe after an intensive six-month review process concludes.
