Greek island tourists trapped by hurricane face rescue as storm intensifies | CNN Business and World News

The fierce wrath of nature is set to strike again in the form of Hurricane Elise, which has already claimed two lives off the coasts near Mykonos. The Greek government issued a warning last night that all vacationers must evacuate immediately as their safety became increasingly compromised amidst escalating weather conditions expected overnight into today.

Evacuees are making for Athens, with thousands of stranded tourists hoping to be rescued within 48 hours from the last reported sighting at midnight near Kalavrti Beach in Mykonos. Weather reports predict a worsening storm as Hurricane Elise escalates into Category 3 by early tomorrow morning, with expected wind speeds reaching up to 120 miles per hour and torrential rainfall causing significant flooding throughout the island's narrow streets.

The National Meteorological Service reports that the storm center will be near Kalavrti Beach later tonight and is projected to move west-northwest, making landfall along mainland Greece's northern Aegean coast by midnight tomorrow night before heading towards Crete within 48 hours.
