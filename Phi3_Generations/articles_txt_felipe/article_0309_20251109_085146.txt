Topic: Style - Beauty

A new trend sweeping through major fashion capitals worldwide has taken center stage in conversations among beauty and style enthusiasts alike: a revival of retro hairstyles from the '70s with an unprecedented twist – eco-consciousness. The return to classic looks like bouffant wigs, big hair rollers set on long shelves by stylists is seen not just as nostalgia but also a fashion statement reflecting current environmental concerns within the beauty industry.

The resurgence first came about last month in Los Angeles during one of Hollywood's biggest red carpet events when actress Emily Rose, known for her effortlessly chic style and strong advocacy for sustainable living, made headlines with a stunning bouffant hairdo reminiscent of the '70s. The bold look not only drew attention from paparazzi but also sparked conversations among attendees about fashion's role in environmental stewardship.

According to stylist Alex Martin, "The past and present are converging on this runway – we see old-school glamour redefined by the consciousness of our times." His statement was echoed earlier when sustainable hairstyling platform 'Green Tresses' released a campaign last year promoting eco-friendly hair products.

"I want to honor those beautiful styles, but I also feel it is my responsibility as an influencer and someone with access to resources," Emily Rose said during the event. "Fashion should not just look good – we must ensure that our choices are kinder to Mother Earth too." Her stylist adds her personal motivation for adopting such a striking hairstyle: "She wanted something unique, but also sustainable and timeless in its essence - hence the eco-conscious wig choice!"

Beauty experts believe that retro styles aren't just about fashion anymore. For many young professionals across major cities like New York or Paris who are increasingly interested in 'sustainable style', this movement represents a broader cultural shift towards reevaluating our consumption habits - not only within the beauty industry but also as consumers at large, says renowned stylist Jean-Luc Dubois.

"There's been significant growth lately among eco-friendly products in every aspect of fashion and design," he points out while reminiscing about a client who asked for an old-school look but with organic materials – "It was then that I realized the world truly stands at this crossroad, redefining style through conscious choices."

As we move forward into spring and summer seasonal events from Cannes Film Festival to Coachella Music & Arts Festival across globe continue in full swing while embracing environmental concerns within fashion – one hairstyle reminiscent of the past could prove transformative for future trends. 

Witnesses say, "I'm so glad we can express ourselves through style without leaving a negative impact on our planet." The new wave in beauty and eco-conscious choices continues to reshape societal norms as people increasingly demand more transparency from the fashion industry when it comes not just styling but also sourcing of materials.
