Topic: Sports - Pro Football

As of Friday afternoon, New York Jets quarterback Sam Darnold has suffered a severe ankle sprain during Sunday's game against Miami Dolphins at MetLife Stadium in East Rutherford on October 3rd. The injury occurred when Darnold attempted to jump-foul as the ball was snapped for fourth down, but instead tripped over his own foot and fell awkwardly onto the turf with immediate visible pain.

Dr. James Smithson of New Jersey Sports Medicine is one of several experts who weighed in on what could be a season-altering injury for Darnold as he begins treatment at The Jets' medical facility within hours after being helped off the field by teammates and coaches, including head coach Adam Gase.

"We had concerns from Day 1 that his footwork was inconsistent," Dr. Smithson says in a telephone interview with CNN Business. "Now we see an injury like this is likely to hinder not just Darnold' hall of fame prospect but also the entire offense."

Jets players are anxiously awaiting word on whether Darnold will require surgery or can return sooner, which has led some speculating if head coach Adam Gase might look elsewhere in his quest for a top quarterback. Assistant Coach Frank Smith provided insight into what Jets officials may be thinking after hours of treatment: "It's too early to tell where this goes but it’ll definitely have an impact on our offense and will likely change the dynamics as we head toward free agency."

As for Darnold, he remains hopeful despite his pain levels being rated at 7 out of 10. "I just want my teammates to know I'm not giving up," said a determined-looking Darnold on Monday afternoon after two days in physical therapy with sports medicine specialist Dr. Susan O’Connor, who emphasized the importance of proper rehabilitation and cautioned that returning too soon could worsen his injury or cause long-term damage to muscles around his ankle: "I'm doing my best here," Darnold said with a smile as he stood up from therapy for short periods when not in pain.

Officials have yet to publicly comment on the situation, but insiders say that this is likely one of their top concerns after two straight years where Jets star quarterback Teddy Mickelson struggled following major injuries and subsequent transfers out midseason before finally ending his career in 2019. A source tells ESPN: "We know we're going to face challenges here with Sam, but our priority is getting him back on the field when he’s ready - both physically and mentally."

With Darnold out for an undetermined period of time, it remains unclear how this impact will play into Jets management decisions as they evaluate players set to enter free agency next week. While some believe their focus should remain on drafting the right talent during 2021's regular season and beyond rather than throwing caution to wind in pursuit of short-term fixes, others argue that such injuries are simply part of life as a professional athlete - especially one who constantly seeks growth: "The Jets need someone with leadership qualities on this roster.
