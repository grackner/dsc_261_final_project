A gourmet experience in Paris that took over a year to plan is now set to become one of France's most anticipated food events for tourists and locals alike; the Grand Bazaar de Cuisine will open its doors next month, offering an eclectic mix of French delicacies alongside international favorites. Set within a historic market building dating back to 1920 located on Rue de la Culture in Montmartre, this grand bazaar brings together over one hundred artisanal cheese makers and gourmet food vendors who will showcase their finest products during the event's week-long run starting June 5th.

The concept of a specialized market such as the Grand Bazaar de Cuisine was born from French confectioner, Marie Dupont, whose love for artisan cheese led her to propose this one-ofinite project during Parisian foodie gatherings last year. "It's about bringing together like-minded individuals who appreciate fine flavors and the traditions that go with them," says Mme. Dupont.
 
Visitors will have a feast for their senses as they walk from stall to stall enjoying handcrafted French Camembert, brie, Roquefort, along with sumptuous delicacies like foie gras terrine and chocolate truffles made by esteemed chefs. "This bazaar not only brings artisans together but also provides an unparalleled opportunity for food lovers to savor the rich tastes of France right here in Paris," adds Chef Jean-Luc Martin, who will be among over thirty renowned culinary experts presenting at the event.
 
The Grand Bazaar de Cuisine is also set to become a hub for food education and cultural exchange with cooking demonstrations scheduled throughout each day of opening week by guest chefs including Chef Emmanuel Petit, known best in his native Bordeaux but recently gaining international acclaim. Attendees can even take part in exclusive wine-pairing sessions curated to enhance the culinary journey through French cuisine with expert sommeliers guiding choices between local wines and rare finds from renowned regions like Burgundy or Champagne.
