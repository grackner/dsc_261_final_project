Topic: Sports - Olympics

Athletes from various countries gathered at Tokyo's Olympic Village to compete in disciplines ranging from gymnastthy and swimming to archery and taekwondo on Tuesday (July 28). The latest round of events saw numerous athletes aiming for their chance at gold, with some hoping to repeat the success they had achieved during preliminary rounds.

Among them was American gymnast Simone Biles who captured a bronze in beam finals after missing out on her initial qualification performance by just five points due to an unfortunate slip of hers that ended up costing her teammate the lead spot, according to USA Gymnastics representatives.

"I was shocked when I saw my score drop from 9.520 (highest possible) on Day One because a technical fall meant losing valuable points," Biles said in an exclusive interview with reporters after her disappointing performance led the team to settle for silver overall, marking their fourth consecutive Olympic podium finish despite never winning gold individually throughout career thus far.

Her coach, Valorie Kondos-Apetolo told CBS Sports News that while Biles is understandably frustrated with not securing another individual title after six World Championship wins and four Olympics medals including two silvers since 2013 when she first joined the US national team at just thirteen years old, but this Olympic experience will still be one thing they can share together for a long time - even if it might not go exactly as planned sometimes.
