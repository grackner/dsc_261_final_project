It was an unexpected turn of events at Tuesday night's All-Star Futures Game in New York City when pitcher Michael Sanchez collapsed on the field during his final out against a high school talent pool team from California. Witnesses reported that after throwing multiple strikes to end the fifth and deciding inning, he suddenly doubled over and struggled for breath before regaining composure just long enough to exit the game drenched but without panic etched on his face as paramedics arrived shortly thereafter. 

"We had a routine start with Sanchez throwing strikes at every attempt until this one," said team doctor Dr. Jane Thompson, who rushed onto the field after hearing cries for help near first base. "When he started cramping up and went down like that -- it's not something we see often."

The 21-year-deceivingly fit right-hander had already pitched in an exhibition game last month at Nationals Park before his historic showing Tuesday night for the New York Yankees, a team he joined after playing minor league baseball with other organizations.
