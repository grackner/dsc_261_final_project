Topic: Style - Beauty

A beauty company has recently launched an unconventional product that aims to tackle acne and other skin blemishes using traditional methods rather than chemical-laden products available in the market today: clay face masks made from organic peas, sweet potatoes, and avocados. The start-up company, "Green Glow Skincare," has already captured attention with its unique selling proposition of combining age-old remedies into a facial treatment designed to clear skin effectively while being kinder to the environment.

The innovative skincare line was first unveiled last week at their new studio located in Brooklyn, New York, where founders Sarah Chen and Mia Rodriguez shared an inspiring vision of revolutionizing beauty care with natural ingredients that are sustainably sourced from local farms. The duo started the project after witnessing firsthand how many chemical products left their skin feeling dry or reacted negatively, driving them to seek a gentler alternative for themselves and others who suffer similar issues.

"When we talk about beauty, it's more than just making your face look good," says Chen during an interview with CNN Style at the launch event last Wednesday evening (April 10). "We want people to understand that taking care of yourself extends beyond superficial aspects and touches on overall well-being."

The founders, who both hail from backgrounds in business management and environmental science respectively before joining forces three years ago, emphasize their commitment towards creating a sustainable brand. To this end, Green Glow Skincare sources its raw materials ethically while ensuring they are packaged using biodegradable containers to reduce the carbon footprint of manufacturing processes as much as possible.

Customers who attended at least one in-studio demonstration were quick to praise their efforts and share testimonials on social media: "Using these face masks has made my complexion softer, brighter, while leaving a natural glow," shared Jenna Murphy after trying the product for just over two weeks. Another customer added that she now feels more confident in her skin thanks to Green Glow's innovative approach toward beauty care with chemical-free products: "I can wear makeup without worrying about it breaking out my face, and I know every ingredient is safe," said Mia Rivera.

As the product quickly gained popularity among New Yorkers seeking more eco-friendly skincare options, Green Glow Skincare's founders are now planning to expand their operations into other major cities across the United States this fall as part of a broader campaign promoting self-love and environmental consciousness in society. "We believe that everyone deserves access to safe products without compromising on quality or ethics," says Rodriguez, reflecting Green Glow's core values during an interview with DailyMail Beauty last Friday (April 15).

With a strong customer base and growing demand for sustainable beauty options from both consumers and retail partners alike in the city, it seems that traditional skincare methods may soon be back as front-runners.
