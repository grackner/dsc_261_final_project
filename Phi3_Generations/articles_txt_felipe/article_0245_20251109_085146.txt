A massive earthquake shook southern India on Saturday morning with a magnitude of 6.8 as per reports from seismic activity monitors in Chennai. The quake struck near the city at approximately 7:52 am local time, causing panic among residents and prompting immediate emergency responses by authorities to ensure public safety.

The tremors were felt across various parts of Tamil Nadu state with initial reports coming from people who described their experiences on social media platforms alongside live updates from the United States Geological Survey (USGS) confirming an epicenter near Puducherry, about 10 miles away from Chennai. Residents in affected areas reported intense shaking lasting for several seconds that caused items to fall off shelves and sparked fears of aftershock activity among the tremor-prone region's populace who are accustomed to seismic events over time.

"The earthquake was strong enough, but luckily I remained safe inside my home with doors firmly shut," shared Mr. Rajan Kumar, a Chennai local and longtime resident of the state, on social media channels where he detailed his account alongside that of several other witnesses who had sought refuge in sturdy buildings across multiple locations within Tamil Nadu.
