Topic: Science - Space

In a remarkable celestial event that delighted skywatchers worldwide last night, an asteroid passed safely near Earth at its closest point of approach within what was deemed to be 'a very narrow window.' The approximately half-mile wide rock named Asteroid #32518 came just 0.04 astronomical units (about 7.9 million kilometers or roughly the distance between Earth and Moon) from our planetary home, offering an unforgettable sight to those with their eyes skyward on April 6th in various locations across North America.


Scientists at NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory quickly analyzed trajectory data and confirmed that Asteroid #32518 would not pose a threat to Earth, according to lead researcher Dr. Emily Johnson: "We closely monitor near-Earth objects but this one is stable with no potential for collision in the next century."


Witnesses at Griffith Observatory on Los Angeles' iconic Hollywood sign hill shared their experiences of seeing streaks and flashes through binoculars, leading to an evening filled with wonder.
