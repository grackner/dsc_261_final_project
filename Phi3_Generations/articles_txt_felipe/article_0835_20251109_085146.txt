Last Saturday evening at half-time during an intense college football matchup between North Carolina Tar Heels and Duke Blue Devils in Durham, North Carolina came a surprising sight: A stray dog found its way onto the field amidst cheers from perplexed spectators. The fluffy golden retriever wandered into the stadium before halftime but remained unbothered by players or fans alike as it roamed freely, attracting waves of soft "awws" and gentle petting among onlookers at half-time's end during a break in play.

Footage captured moments later showed Duke Coach Mike Cummings walking through the stands toward where he thought the dog had exited before reentering as it trotted back onto field, seemingly unfazed by its brief detour from human activity within stadium walls — or perhaps even daring to do so.

"It's unbelievable," said an onlooker who asked for only her first name and wished not to be identified further in the media coverage of this unusual occurrence at Bryte Gym, Durham. "I thought he must have escaped somehow when I came out here earlier today."
