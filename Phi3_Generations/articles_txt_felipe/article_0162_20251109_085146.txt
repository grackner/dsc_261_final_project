Brian Boitani and his son-in-law Brian Hosford were arrested at a New York marathon charity event last Saturday night as reported by The DailyMail.com in the heart of Times Square, when they allegedly attempted to take part despite having no qualifying times themselves. Officials quickly intervened after noticing them among experienced runners and took immediate action based on local regulations prohibiting unqualified participants from running. 

According to race director Karen Simmons, "We have a policy that allows those with certified time trials or personal best records the opportunity to compete in various categories at our charity races." Boitani and Hosford failed to meet these standards but still decided to participate. 

Runners were disqualified when officials identified them as non-qualifiers, per NYPD rules that enforce such measures during major city events like the marathon in Times Square aimed at supporting charitable causes. "It's our duty as organizers and participants alike to uphold fairness," Simmons added.
