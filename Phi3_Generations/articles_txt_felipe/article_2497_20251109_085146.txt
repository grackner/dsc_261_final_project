A devastating fire swept through a historic building in Nigeria's capital city of Abuja on Saturday evening, claiming at least seven lives and leaving hundreds homeless. The blaze broke out around 7:0 enduring inferno gutted the centuries-old Mbari House at about 9 p.m., with smoke billowing into the sky for hours afterward as firefighters battled to bring it under control.

This tragedy unfolded in Abuja's Garki district, a bustling neighborhood known for its vibrant culture and rich history. The Mbari House is considered an architectural gem of the city; built-in 1743 during the reign of Oba Dimi Agomoh II, it served as both his palace and spiritual center where he performed sacred rites to honor Iya or ancestors with intricate carvings depicting their deeds on wooden panels.

"The Mbari House is a symbol not just for our past but also represents the spirit of communal living that was so integral in Nigerian history," said historian Dr. Uchenna Onyebadi, who has studied traditional structures extensively and now works with UNESCO on preserving such landmarks across Africa."Its loss to this fire will be felt deeply by many here."

Witness Ebenezer Okonzo described seeing flames engulfing the ancient structure from his nearby home. "It looked like it was burning down before our eyes," he said tearfully while speaking with local media outlets on Sunday morning. "The firemen were trying their best but, I guess we all know what happened."
