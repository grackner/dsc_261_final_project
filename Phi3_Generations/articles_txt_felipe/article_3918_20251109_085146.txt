Topic: Business - Tech

Four major tech companies have announced a partnership to develop next-genereneration wireless charging technology expected to revolutionize how we charge our devices on the go. Apple Inc., Google LLC, Samsung Electronics Co., and Qualcomm Incorporated signed an agreement today in Silicon Valley with plans for commercialization by 2025 after extensive research conducted at their respective headquarters in California since last year's breakthrough announcement of a prototype wireless charging system.

The companies have dedicated years to the development, testing and refining this new technology which promises much faster charging times without compromising battery life or requiring any special hardware from consumer devices like coils currently used in existing induction-based systems. As part of today's announcement Apple CEO Tim Cook said: "Today marks a pivotal moment for all tech enthusiasts eager to see how wireless charging can evolve, and we are thrilled that these four industry leaders have come together with their unmatched expertise."

In addition to faster charging times at more locations simultaneously than current systems offer, this partnership plans on creating an open-source protocol allowing any smartphone or device manufacturer access. In the early testing phase where devices could be charged in seconds instead of minutes over a distance of up to 10 feet away from their power source using just standard electromagnetic fields - unlike traditional wired connections which require direct physical contact between chargers and cables, making them prone for damage or wear-and-tear.

Samsung spokesperson Kim Jungwoo said: "Our goal is to make wireless more convenient than ever before without compromising our consumers' beloved devices." He also added that the team hopes this new technology can help minimize electronic waste and lower ecological impact associated with daily charging habits.
