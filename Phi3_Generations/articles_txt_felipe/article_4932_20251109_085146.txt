Topic: World - Europe

A massive earthquake measuring a magnitude of 7.8 hit Italy late Monday night near Friuli Venezia Giulia region close to Slovenian border and Montenegro coastline at about 10:50 pm local time (UTC+2). The quake prompted immediate evacuations from affected areas, with over two thousand individuals reported as displaced.


"The tremors were felt across multiple countries; we're coordinating relief efforts," said EU Civil Protection Coordinator Thalia Katsoumani to a group of concerned citizens in the epicenter city of Udine. "We have sent rescue teams and are working with local authorities."


Emergency services were swiftly on scene, but preliminary reports suggest that there has been significant damage to infrastructure including roads, bridges, railway lines, and power networks within a radius encompassing the town of Pordenone. The earthquake struck just 12 miles from Lake Garda' end at Udine Airport with seismic activity recorded as far away as Croatia and Albania.


"The aftershocks are still frequent," stated Dr. Marco Silvestri, a geologist stationed in Italy who has studied the region for decades. "We need to monitor these tremors closely." Local historian Elisa Bianchi commented on past earthquakes affecting Udine and surrounding towns: "These areas have faced seismic activity before; this is not new but always devastating nonetheless."


In a statement, Italian Prime Minister Mario Draghi said that the government was taking all necessary measures to ensure people's safety.
