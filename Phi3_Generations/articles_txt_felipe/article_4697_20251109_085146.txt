China announces new economic measures to curb housing bubble concerns | The CNN Business News Team May 5th at a press conference in Beijing by Chinese Communist Party officials announced an unprecedented series of interventions designed to address growing fears over the stability of its property market. President Xi Jinping, Premier Li Keqiang, and other senior policymakers disclosed plans including increased taxes on speculative land purchases across major cities such as Shanghai, Beijing, and Guangzhou from September 2023 to January 2dealing with the nation's historical housing affordability issues.

"We recognize that our citizens are calling for more responsible home ownership policies," Premier Li stated in his opening remarks at the conference held yesterday morning alongside a flurry of supporting documents outlining specific fiscal strategies and targets expected to curb speculative buying practices significantly over two years, according to state media reports.

Central government regulators have long been tightening controls on real estate markets since early 2013 due in part blamed for the nation's inflated property prices but avoided direct measures until now fearing broader economic implications of previous interventions led by former President Hu Jintao during his tenure (2002-2012). A senior official at China Banking Regulatory Commission warned today that while there was still much work to be done, these new policies represented important steps toward financial stability and home ownership rights: "By creating more affordable options for citizens through various incentives - we hope they will see their futures beyond simply owning a property."

Economists have expressed mixed reactions so far with some applauding the government's bold move while others doubt its effectiveness, citing past failed attempts at similar regulatory interventions. Chinese state media quoted Xu Jianyong from Tsinghua University who said that although there were concerns about potential negative impact on housing prices and construction activities: "It is critical for the government to manage this transition carefully so as not to disrupt overall economic growth."

Local governments across China also agreed they will need extra funding resources alongside these national measures while acknowledging local economies would take time recover from any initial decline in real estate investment.
