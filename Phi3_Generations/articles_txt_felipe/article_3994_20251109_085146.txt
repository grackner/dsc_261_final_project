Born to enigmatic origins, street artist Jonathan Reece—also known as Banksy—has spent years using public spaces around London where his politically-charged and socially conscious artwork resonates strongly across generations. Inspired by the world he inhabits through a gritty lens of social commentary laced with hope, it seems Reece has now ventured into an entirely new realm—a bold fusion between street graffiti techniques and high-fashion runway sensibilities that many say could be indicative of Banksy's next major evolution as a cultural force.
 
"Banksy’s work, always provocative yet timelessly relevant in the face of social issues," said art historian Michael Thompson during an interview with Reuters last Thursday afternoon ahead of his exhibition appearance at MoMA where he previewed Banksy's latest installment titled "Past Meets Present."
 
With a background deeply rooted within urban London culture dating back to the late '90s, artist Jonathan Reece started performing unauthorized artwork in public spaces before moving onto larger exhibitions across Europe. His distinct style utilizing sharp political and social commentary along with stunning visual imagery became widely recognized throughout England's capital city where his street pieces remain powerful representations of the complex relationship between society, history, and individual identity within a modern context—a dynamic that Banksy has sought to capture in this latest venture.
