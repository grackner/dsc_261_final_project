Davis High School football team suffered a heartbreaking defeat in Saturday night's championship game against Oakwood Prep at Lucas Field. In an upset that took fans and pundits by surprise, the underdog Davis squad fell short to their opponents with just six minutes remaining when they could have tied or won the matchup 24-21 after a controversial play involving offensive lineman Alex Thompson was reviewed for potential holding.


"We thought we had it until that whistle," said coach Mike Larson, reflecting on what many saw as an unjust call against Davis's last drive of the game right before halftime after Oakwood blocked a critical fourth-down snap with two seconds left in regulation time. "The entire team felt like we had earned it."


Davis rallied back into contention following several successful drives, but missed opportunities and an uncharacteristically aggressive first half defense from their opponents sealed the fate for Davis High's star players. Fans in attendance expressed shock at the outcome while many social media accounts were filled with debates over officiating decisions throughout both teams' histories of rivalry dating back two decades to a game that ended 45-32, favoring Oakwood Prep since then.
