Topic: World - Africa

In a groundbredependent move for African development, Ethiopia has received an unexpected $500 million pledge from Norway's state-owned oil company, StatoilHydro ASA (now Equinor), aimed at expanding the country’s energy infrastructure and creating job opportunities. The announcement was made on Wednesday in Oslo by Ethiopian Prime Minister Hailemariam Desalegn during a high-level economic summit.

Hailemariam expressed his gratitude towards Norway's unprecedented support, which will aid the Horn of Africa nation to bolster its economy and develop sustainably over time: “This is not only an investment in Ethiopia’s oil industry but also a testament to our commitment towards building greener future for all.”

StatoilHydro CEO Eldar Sælen added, "Investing in Africa's energy sector has the potential of transformative impact on people and economies across this continent. We look forward working with Ethiopia as they develop their rich natural resources." 

The funds will be allocated towards modernizing power plants and exploring new oil fields to enhance both domestic use and exportation capabilities in line with Africa’s growing energy demand while reducing carbon emissions by transitioning more toward renewable sources. The development project is set to commence within the next fiscal year, 2017-2018.

Experts believe that such investments can lead to significant strides towards poverty eradication and social advancement in a nation struggling with an expanding population exceeding 95 million people. Ethiopia’s energy sector currently supplies only about half of the country's electricity needs, according to Addisu Tarefu, senior director at IHS Energy: “We need massive investment now more than ever if we want to meet growing demand and decrease reliance on hydro power alone which is susceptible due to climatic changes.”

Reactions from Ethiopian citizens have been overwhelmingly positive. Meklit Abebe, a resident of Addis Ababa shared that “this investment could bring hope for the better life we deserve as Africans and will help bridge many economic gaps in our country." 

In light of this remarkable deal, neighboring African nations are keen to replicate Ethiopia's success. The Minister for Foreign Affairs of Tanzania recently announced plans to form an international consortium with other East African countries interested in similar projects. As Africa continues on its path towards sustainable growth and stability, it seems investments like this one could potentially be game-changers not just locally but throughout the continent.
