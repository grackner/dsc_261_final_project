Topic: Business - Tech

Asmaa Khan and her brother Mohammed Shahid Al-Harthi were arrested yesterday in Ajman city for hacking into a financial institution's system to gather information on investment portfolios worth $10 million USD, authorities confirmed today (June 5). The tech duo had been monitored by local IT experts and government agencies since April when suspicious activities began at Al-Khaleej Bank.

Asmaa Khan said that their motivation was to expose potential corruption within the bank's investment practices, but they failed to anticipate a swift response from law enforcement: "We were hoping for an open conversation and some transparency," Asmaa told reporters today outside Ajman police headquarters. Mohammed Shahid Al-Harthi added that their intentions weren't criminal as such, noting, “There was something fundamentally wrong here.”

Local IT specialists reported observing unusual login patterns originating from the Khan family’s residence near Sheikh Khazal Road in Ajman city around mid-April. Authorities received multiple tips linking Asmaa and Mohammed Shahid to illicit activities, which were corroborated by digital forensic analysis pointing towards unauthorized access into Al-Khaleej Bank's secure network on several occasions in May.

The duo was apprehended as they tried to enter a meeting room at the bank undercover last Tuesday (May 26), where representatives of financial institutions and government regulators were presenting their findings about industry practices related investment opportunities within UAE's economy, which are now subjected to scrutiny.

Chief Investigator Sheikh Abdullah Al-Shaali confirmed the arrests but declined comment on any possible charges: "The investigation is still in progress," he said outside his office today where Asmaa Khan and her brother have been taken into custody at Ajman police headquarters pending further clarification.

Officials say they are hopeful that this incident will bring about necessary reforms within financial institutions, especially those offering investment portfolios in UAE markets: "We need to ensure greater accountability and transparency," remarked a senior official from the Dubai Financial Services Authority.
