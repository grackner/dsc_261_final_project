"We understand this is an unprecedented situation," says John Smith, President and CEO of Allied Global Travels in London. “It will take time for our industry as a whole—but we’ll weather the storm.”

The policy stems from escalating concerns about variant transmission risks amidst holiday travel spikes seen over recent weeks worldwide – particularly notable is Europe, where France and Germany have reported rising cases in their respective nations. “In order to keep people safe,” says World Health Organization representative Emma Greenfield, who emphasizes the importance of complying with new protocols as countries struggle against evolving threats from COVID-19 variants.

As international travel has resumed cautiously since last year's lockdown measures subsided globally—with many airlines introducing health and safety guidelines to protect passengers on board—it seems the industry, now forced into a recalibration in an age of uncertainty around COVID-19. This development follows news from New York City yesterday where Mayor Bill DeBlasio urged citizens not "to cancel holiday travel plans," but rather encouraged them take necessary precautions and adhere to new quarantine rules upon arrival, adding another layer of complexity for city visitors in the days ahead—with more information still being gathered about specifics.

Reactions from airlines have been mixed: American Airlines reports a slight drop in bookings since today's announcement but expects demand will return over time as travelers become familiar with this new norm, while Delta Air Lines has expressed concern for how mandatory quarantines may impact their competitive positioning and customer service standards going forward. Meanwhile tourism boards worldwide have been scrambling to put in place virtual alternatives like immersive web-based tours – some countries such as Italy even reinstating travel restrictions with a two weeks' lockdown mandate for arriving international travellers, reports the European Travel Commission (ETC).

As of press time this afternoon, officials continue to refine details on implementation procedures and how these changes will be communicated in multiple languages across their respective airports. "It is clear that we are facing significant hurdles," says Dr. Anne Chang from Harvard University's Public Health Department who specializes in epidemiology of pandemics—she notes the importance for governments to ensure protocols do not discourage necessary travel and maintain confidence amongst populations during this trying time while staying focused on public safety as a priority above all else.
