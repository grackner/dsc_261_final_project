Jamie Foxx has announced his latest acting role in a highly anticipated Hollywood blockbuster set to release on March 15th next year at Universal Studios Backlot and Warner Bros.' Los Angeles lot. The Oscar-winning actor, known for his versatile performances across various film genres, will step into the shoes of an enigmatic character in a story that promises both mystery and action amidst its star-studded cast.

“It’s been three years since I last worked on such an ambitious project,” Foxx told reporters during yesterday's red carpet event where he made his appearance for the first time after signing onto this new role in 2021. “I can confidently say that I don't want to stop until we uncover all of its secrets.”

The film, which has been titled 'Midnight Enigma', will be directed by acclaimed director Christopher Nolan and features some well-known names such as Scarlett Johannson, Idris Elba, Robert Pattinson, Rebecca Ferguson and a cameo from Morgan Freeman. The film's script was written primarily by Jonathan Olshey who has worked with Foxx before on 'Django Unchained'.

The news follows several years since the last time Foxx had committed himself to a leading role in an established franchise. The actor’s latest project represents his first significant engagement with Warner Bros and marks a notable reunion for him at Los Angeles-based studios as he was once based out of Universal Studios backlot during some early phases of 'Django Unchained'.

"This is going to be one of those films that I hope you can see from the very beginning, right after it drops," Foxx said. "I want every fan and critic alike to experience this story in its raw, uncut form." 

In related news yesterday at a press conference with fellow cast members including Scarlett Johannson: 'Midnight Enigma' Director Christopher Nolan shared some tantalizing details of the film’s plot. "What we have created here is not just an entertaining story," said Nolan, "(it) explores themes that are universal and timeless – love, trust, betrayal.” 

Nolan continued to stress on his directorial intentions: “I want audiences worldwide to be part of our journey. To unravel the mysteries with us." While Nolan has not disclosed a release date yet for 'Midnight Enigma', Warner Bros and Universal Studios have already announced their film's premiere on March 15th next year, which will mark Foxx’ return to acting in one of Hollywood's biggest franchises since ‘Django Unchained'.

Foxx is expected back at the Los Angeles set from February through April for additional shooting as Nolan plans some extra action scenes and special effects that are sure to keep audiences on their edge. 

Jamie Foxx has been re-signed by Universal Studios with an exclusive first look deal inked last summer, after parting ways professionally at Warners following the release of Django Unchained back in 2013.
