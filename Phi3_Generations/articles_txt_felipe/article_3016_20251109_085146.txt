Topic: World - Middle East

Amid rising tensions in Yemen' enduring civil war since 2014 and heightened regional rivalries on February 3rd, a significant escalation occurred when Houthis launched missiles towards Saudi Arabia’s border town of Najran. The launch came hours after UAE-backed Hadi government forces recaptured territories previously held by the Iran-aligned rebels in Yemen's southern provinces on January 29th, a move that has been widely viewed as an attempt to regain lost ground and assert military dominance over rival factions.


The missile attack reportedly originated from Al Khalafah air base located near the Houthi-controlled city of Yar al Batnah in the northern part of Yemen, around 50 kilometers (31 miles) southwest of Najran according to eyewitness accounts. The Saudi Arabian Ministry of Defense confirmed that no civilian casualties were reported as a result of this particular incident but warned citizens in border areas about potential threats on February 4th, while also expressing gratitude for their resilience during the previous weeks' escalating conflict which have taken lives and displaced thousands.


"The recent attack against Najran underscores our commitment to safeguarding national security in this unstable region," said Mohammad Al-Sulami, a spokesperson for Saudi Arabia’s Foreign Ministry on February 5th. "We extend sincere condolences and support to the people of Najran as they continue to rebuild their lives amidst these challenging times."


A local farmer who witnessed part of the launch described seeing multiple ballistic projectiles streaking through Yar al Batnah's sky, leaving an eerie silence among his fellow villagers before returning back into enemy territory. He also noted that Houthi forces have been increasingly aggressive in recent months, prompting a renewed sense of urgency for the international community to resolve this conflict which has led to famine and cholera outbreaks across Yemen due largely to years-long blockade imposed by regional actors.


"There is no quick fix when it comes down to resolving issues in our nation," said Hadi's political adviser, Abdullah Al-Baidi on February 6th after meeting with several foreign diplomats at the Riyadh Embassy where he stressed his government’s commitment for peace talks as a means of ending this devastating war.


The United Nations reported that since last September, armed clashes and missile attacks have escalated between Yemen's two front-line forces – the UAE/Saudi Arabian backed government led by Hadi against Shia militant Houthis with Iran as their main ally. This complex conflict has spiraled into what many observers are calling a proxy war, drawing in nations beyond the Middle East and prompting international calls for diplomatic solutions to quell further escalation of hostilities which have already left thousands dead while millions more displaced or internally fleeing as refugees within Yemen.
