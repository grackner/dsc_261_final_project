After Hours in Tokyo: Stock Market Drops Significantly on Tuesday Evening

Tokyo's bustling business district was not as lively this weekend evening when Japan'in stock market took an unexpected plunge, closing down by a notable margin. On Monday night between 6 and 7 PM local time (midnight-1 AM EST), the Nikkei Stock Exchange in Tokyo fell more than two percent after hours on Tuesday following revelations of widespread accounting irregularities within several key sectors, including technology and energy industries.

According to sources close to major companies implicated by these discrepancies such as Mitsubishi Electric Corp., Panasonic Group Corporation, Tokyo Electric Power Company Holdings Inc., the issues appear linked to misrepresentation of fiscal results in order to meet investor expectations for continuous growth.

"We are thoroughly investigating all accounts and financial statements related to these irregularities," said Takashi Kawano, chief executive officer (CEO) at Tokyo Electric Power Company Holdings Inc., a company that reportedly overstated its earnings by around 5 billion yen ($45 million). "It's our top priority right now."

The Nikkei 225 average fell to close below the psychologically important level of two thousand seven hundred points last night after suffering from an intraday drop exceeding three percent at times. The downward momentum carried into Tuesday morning, with Tokyo's benchmark stock index remaining in negative territory by mid-morning (3:00 AM EST).

Reactions on Wall Street have been cautious as traders weigh the ramifications of these revelations and their potential to impact broader markets. "A lot will depend upon how deep this goes into Japanese companies that do business with us here in America," said Jake Cohen, head trader at Chicago's Century Securities Company Inc., a firm dealing primarily on NASDAQ-listed stocks but which holds some Nikkei shares as well.

In an effort to reassure shareholders and restore faith among investors in the long run, many companies have pledged transparency throughout this investigation process while maintaining that they will take appropriate corrective measures once all facts are confirmed through rigorous audits conducted by third-party firms. "We understand your concerns regarding recent news reports about some of our financial statements and we want to assure you; everything here is above board," said Yoshiyuki Takahashi, president at Mitsubishi Electric Corp., who added his company would be cooperating fully with government officials in their investigation.
