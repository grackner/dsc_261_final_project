Los Angeles Dodgers outfielder Yasiel Puig hit a walk-off home run in yesterday's game against San Francisco Giants at Dodger Stadium on April 10th. The victory has propelled the Los Angeles team to an early lead as they enter the All-Star break with a record of 56 wins and 37 losses, placing them first place within their division for the third consecutive season under new manager Dave Roberts' tenure since October 2018 when he replaced Don Mattingly.

Puig, who was selected as an All-Star reserve player this year with a .954 OPS (On-Base Plus Slugging) and led off all home runs for the Dodgers in April's games so far, provided his signature performance last night after fouling one pitch out of play. "I knew tonight was about doing something special," Puig said with a smile as he approached third base post batting practice earlier this week to work on improving power-hitting techniques from new hitting coach Matt Drews and MLB's own video analysis department.

As fans erupted in cheers when Yasiel hit the ball over the left field fence after a walk, 24-year-debutante Andrew Moore of San Francisco Giants said he was thoroughly impressed by Puig's power at his debut game against Los Angeles Dodgers last night. "Puig showed great composure and skill today," shared Moores from the bench behind home plate during a press conference in front of Sunset Park, Brooklyn shortly after noon ET when questioned about how he would be able to handle such talent on his own team's roster next season if given the opportunity.
