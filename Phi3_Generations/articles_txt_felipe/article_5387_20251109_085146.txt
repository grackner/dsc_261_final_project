Topic: Business - After-Hours

New York City's Financial District had its most active evening of stock trading since September as market participants celebrated a robust economic forecast with an aggressive buying strategy post-earnings season report on Tuesday night, reports CNNMoney. According to sources within the industry and preliminary data from major exchanges, New York Stock Exchange experienced unprecedented volumes of trading after regular hours as investors took advantage of lower share prices following earnings announcements earlier in the day, leading some analysts to speculate that a market correction might be on its way. 


The unexpected rally across various sectors had both optimists and pessimists abuzz within trading floors from Wall Street's iconic buildings down through SoHo late into Wednesday evening, as the New York Stock Exchange (NYSE) witnessed over $10 billion in after-hours trades alone. "It has been an extraordinary day with buying surges across multiple sectors," said Chris Johnson, head of trading at Goldman Sachs Group Inc., which is one of the largest investment banks that operates as a market maker on Wall Street alongside competitors like JP Morgan Chase & Co. and Citigroup Inc.. "Investors are looking for value in these companies after taking losses earlier this week."


Experts had mixed feelings about whether such late-night trading binge would last long, warning of potential overextension due to the rapid movement on a typically quiet evening as markets close at 4:00 p.m., according to Bloomberg's report from Tuesday night activity. "The market is clearly seeing some optimism in certain sectors but I suspect we may see sellers step up soon if they sense an overextension," said Alexandra Dapolito, a portfolio manager at Van Eck Associates Corp., which manages $31 billion and has both bullish and bearish positions across industries.
