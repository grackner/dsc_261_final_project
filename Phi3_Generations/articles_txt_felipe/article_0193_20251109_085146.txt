New Sustainable Clothing Line Launches on Main Street in Brooklyn This Spring

As spring blossoms across New York City streets this year with more colors and life than ever before, a new fashion line is debuting right at the heart of style-conscious neighborhoods. The eco-friendly clothing collection from sustainable brand 'Green Thread' will hit local boutiques in Brooklyn on May 5th as part of their expansion into urban retail spaces to promote environmentally responsible fashion among city dwellers and visitors alike.

"The launch is not just about introducing a new line, it’s setting an example for conscious consumerism,” says founder Amara Singh, who has been working with organic materials sourced from ethical suppliers worldwide since 2termlaunchin18 months ago in Los Angeles. Now expanding her vision to New York City's fashion scene and beyond, she hopes this will inspire other brands as well.

The brand’s signature attire—ranging from comfortable yet elegant tunics made of recycled organic cotton for casual wear days all the way up to sleek blazers using sustainable leather alternatives – aims at redefining how we think about style without harming our planet. The collection also incorporates locally-sourced fabrics, as Singh believes in supporting local artisans and communities while reducing carbon footprint from transportation.

“I'm excited to bring this line here because I believe fashion needs a serious conversation around sustainability,” says the London native whose parents moved her family back to their birth country at age 10 after growing up as successful actors in India before relocating firstly New York City and then Los Angeles for career opportunities themselves. “Being from an immigrant background, I understand that cultural fashion can sometimes feel like a minefield when it comes to balancing heritage traditions with modern ethical concerns.”

Singh has already partnered up with Brooklyn boutique 'Urban Elegance', known for curating hand-picked collections from emerging designers each season, as well as New York's popular sustainable fashion platform ‘Green Outfit’ who plans to highlight the brand during their city street shows this year.

“This new line is fantastic and something that we are looking forwarded with great anticipation,” says owner of Urban Elegance Rachel Levin, a Brooklyn native in her mid-40s whose career as an environmental consultant for major fashion brands has led to several sustainable collaborations throughout the city. “We’re excited about working alongside such passionate and purposeful designers who are truly making waves by challenging industry standards with their forward thinking creativity.”

The 'Green Thread' collection is also set to feature in a special event hosted at one of New York City’s iconic art galleries, The Met Cloisters located off the main road on Manhattan Island. Art curator and designer Julia Soto expresses how she believes this collaboration between fashion designers who share her vision can further promote sustainability awareness amongst their audience - “I am so thrilled to bring together world-class art, culture and environmentally conscious clothing here at The Met Cloisters,” said the Mexican born New Yorker.
