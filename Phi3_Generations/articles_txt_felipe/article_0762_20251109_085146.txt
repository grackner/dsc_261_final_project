"Boston residents grappling with a series of unexpected snowstorms began their weekend yesterday when an arctic blast swept through Massachusetts on Tuesday morning. Over the course of three days since last Saturday, Boston experienced record-breaking low temperatures and relentless winter weather that forced local schools to shut down temporarily out of concern for students' safety.


The harsh conditions struck when forecasters predicted a mild day with chances of rain turning into cold showers by late afternoon on Tuesday, prompting city officials to advise caution among the populace and prepare emergency services accordingly. Mayor Jane Smith stated in an early morning press conference that 'we've been fortunde enough this year not to be caught off guard again.'


The snow started as a light flurry around 6:00 AM but quickly escalated into heavy, wet flakes by midday. By Saturday afternoon, the accumulation reached an unprecedented three feet of whiteout conditions across various neighborhoods, most notably in South Boston and Charlestown where snowdrifts grew to impressive heights beyond city sight lines.
