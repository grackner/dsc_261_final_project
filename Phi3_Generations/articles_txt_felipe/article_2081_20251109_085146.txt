Topic: US - Crime + Justice

As of Thursday morning in Chicago, Illinois, reports have surfaced detailing a significant rise in petty crime rates throughout March and April amidst widespstatic COVID-19 pandemic fears that saw many residents stay indoors for extended periods. According to local law enforcement data released today by the Chicago Police Department (CPD), there has been an uptick of 20% in burglaries, a rise of nearly 35% in vehicle break-ins and approximately doubled incidents of retail fraud within these months alone.

Chief Inspector Raymond O'Connor shared his concern: "We are seeing more people resorting to crime due to desperation caused by the lockdown measures." He also urged residents not to let fear dictate their behavior, advising them instead on preventative safety strategies when they must go out for essential errands.

Ex-convict and reformed citizen Tonya Robinson was among those affected during this time frame: "I needed money more than ever as I lost my job because of the pandemic," she said while sitting in her now empty apartment, forced to vacate after several unsuccessful burglary attempts.
