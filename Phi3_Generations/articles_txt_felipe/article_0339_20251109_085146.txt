Topic: World - India

In Mumbai, a fire engulfed an iconic heritage building last Thursday evening, igniting concerns among residents and conservationists about the safety of India's historic edifices. The five-story Art Deco structure at Ghatkeshwar Street in South Mumbai caught fire around 7:30 PM local time on March 15th after a short circuit caused an electrical failure.


"The building has stood for over half a century, and it's imperative to understand the implications of such incidents," said Dr. Anand Rao, cultural heritage expert at Mumbai University. The fire was brought under control within two hours with minimal structural damage but led to worries about similar buildings in the densely-packed metropolis where many historic properties exist close together and rely on aged electrical systems for lighting and climate control.
