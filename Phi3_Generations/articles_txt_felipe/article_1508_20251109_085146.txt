Closures came as a shock to countless vacation-goers and residents alike who were looking forward to enjoying their time in one of America's most famous cities for luxury hospitality, entertainment options such as the famed 'Neon Empire', Broadway shows like "Wicked", or simply lounging by pools on sunny evenings.

"This is one of the most serious issues we've faced in quite some time," said Mayor Carolyn Goodman who had repeatedly assured residents and tourists alike about their safety standards on Las Vegas Boulevard over decades as a seasoned politician now at her post since 2015, during an emergency city council meeting.

"This incident has left the hospitality industry reeling," stated Randy Winston, president of Clark County's tourism board which reported annual hotel revenues in Las Vegas exceeding $4 billion last year alone with millions more pouring into gaming and entertainment sectors annually as per a count from July 2018.
