The Philippine government has reported numerous casualties as of now—numbers that are likely to swell in coming days due to aftershocks continuously rocking the region which have already hampered rescue efforts. According to local emergency response officials, initial estimates stand around 150 confirmed fatalities with dozens still missing and thousands displaced from their homes on Luzon Island alone; a staggering testament considering it is home to nearly one-seventh of the country's population.

"This was beyond anything I could have prepared for," says Maria Del Carmen, who along with her family has spent 48 hours in improvised shelter following their village being swept away by mudslides triggered by seismic shockwaves; a common aftermath of the quakes as experts note.
