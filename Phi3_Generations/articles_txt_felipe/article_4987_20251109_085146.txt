The incident occurred around dusk as hundreds reported a mysterious glow high up in the sky while walking back home from work or school on Monday night of last week.

A senior official with India's space research organization, Vikram Nehru, stated that despite preliminary examinations ruling out any potential misfired satellites as plausible explanations for these phenomena observed in the capital region and neighboring areas on July 15th, no definitive cause has been established yet. "This is something quite unusual we're dealing with here," Nehru explained during a press conference at New Delhi's central railway station yesterday afternoon (July 16).

Many eyewitnesses recounted seeing the object change color, speed up or slow down and even seemed to hover momentarily before disappearing out of sight - all while defying conventional understanding about how objects should behave in Earth’s atmosphere. Dr. Priya Agarwal who claimed she saw a bright red blinking light just above her head when she was crossing the Yamuna river, shared her experience: "I couldn't believe my eyes; it looked like something out of sci-fi movies."

Astrophysicist and retired professor Dr. Amit Malhotra commented on social media platforms expressing his views about this strange occurrence saying that while such phenomena remain a mystery to mankind, they might simply represent natural atmospheric occurrences or unexplained human-made objects which science hasn't yet grasped due to its complexity - emphasizing not jumping into speculation without enough data.

Echoing this sentiment is Professor Rajeev Sinha from the Aeronautics and Space Engineering department at Jamia Millia Islamia University in New Delhi, who cautioned against drawing quick conclusions about what might be happening above us stating that "It's critical we maintain calm until more facts are unearthed."

Indian Air Force officials have confirmed that they would intensify surveillance over the area to gather further information and identify any potential threats or risks. They will employ high-power radars, infrared tracking systems as well as aircraft patrols in a bid not only for scientific curiosity but also national security purposes until this baffling phenomenon is explained satisfactorily - an assurance given by Air Chief Marshal Rakesh Virk after the briefing yesterday afternoon.
