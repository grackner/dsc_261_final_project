Topic: Tech - Innovative Cities

In a groundbredependent move toward sustainability and innovation, Silicon Valley's San Jose has announced its latest project to become one of America’s most forward-thinking urban areas by the year 2045. The city plans are fueled by an initiative called 'Green Horizon', aiming at transforming transportation with a fleet of self-driving electric vehicles (EV) as early as next summer, reports CNN on Tuesday, March 13th.

San Jose Mayor Sam Liccardo expressed his optimism for the project: "We are determined to become an innovative city where technology and sustainability walk hand in hand," he said during yesterday's press conference at City Hall. The self-driving EV fleet is set to replace 10% of San Jose’s current transportation system, providing a glimpse into the future as we know it while reducing carbon footprint and cutting down on traffic congestion – issues that have plagued Silicon Valley for years.

According to data from the National Renewable Energy Laboratory (NREL), San Jose's ambitious project will result in a reduction of greenhouse gas emissions by 20%, based solely on these self-driving electric vehicles being deployed throughout city limits, which have an estimated population nearing half a million.

During yesterday’s press conference at City Hall, NREL's Director Dr. Linda Martin said: "The introduction of autonomous EV fleet will not only help with the air quality but also promote clean energy and sustainability as our city continues to grow." As part of San Jose’s Green Horizon initiative, other eco-friendly projects are being planned including solar power installations on municipal buildings, tree planting campaign for carbon sequestration purposes along major highways, and a comprehensive recycling program.

Local businesses in the tech industry have already expressed enthusiasm toward San Jose's Green Horizon initiative: "We see it as not just an innovative step forward but also one that would make doing business here even better," said Tomas Vargas, CEO of TechSolutions Corp.

As for how the public will react to these sweeping changes — well so far they’ve been generally positive during a city survey conducted last month and with growing interest in environmental sustainability across all sectors nationwide— it appears San Jose is set on its course as one of America's most innovative, green cities by 2045.
