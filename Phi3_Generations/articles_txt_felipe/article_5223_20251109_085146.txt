In a remarkable turn of events in Egypt's capital city Cairo on March 15th, President Abdel Fattah el-Sisi announced his intention to resign effective immediately amid growing public discontent and health concerns due to the COVID-19 pandemic. The unprecedent decision came as he addressed thousands gathered for a national ceremony in Tahrir Square celebrating women's empowerment, which was then hastily adjourned following el-Sisi's unexpected announcement from his podium atop a horse cart adorned with Egyptian flags.

"It is not by choice that I am resigning today but under the great weight of my conscience and love for our beloved nation," stated President Abdel Fattah El-Sisi in an impromptu speech broadcast on national television networks across the country, leaving observers stunned. Egypt's Foreign Minister Khaled Azzouni echoed his sentiments: "Our respected leader has decided to resign out of a sense of duty and responsibility towards our people during this difficult time."

The decision follows months of escalating protests against economic hardships, high unemployment rates, political corruption allegations as well as growing criticism about how the Egyptian government handled COVID-19 restrictions. Many believe that his resignation is a response to these mounting pressures from all fronts - social and medical alike.

According to health expert Dr. Ahmed Morsi at Cairo's Al-Ahram National Hospital, "The burden on our hospitals has become overwhelming due to the recent surge in COVID-19 cases." He highlighted that Egypt reported approximately 20,000 new daily confirmed cases last week alone.

El-Sisi's announcement drew immediate reactions from various quarters of society - some hailed it as a step towards democracy while others saw potential instability in his departure at this critical time for the nation already facing economic challenges and ongoing political unrest dating back to 2013. "We applaud President el-Sisi's decision, which reflects humility amid crisis," tweeted Ahmed Ibrahim, a prominent activist who has been advocating democratic reforms in Egypt for years.
