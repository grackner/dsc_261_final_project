Reports indicate that supporters linked directly to national parties are among those accused by witnesses who observed intimidating behavior or attempts to block people from voting in certain precincts across the five boroughs—particularly Queens and Brooklyn, where margins between candidates running against each other could be particularly tight.

"I was told that I might not vote unless my name changed on a paper ballot," says Michael Thompson, 68-year-old retiree from Bushwick in Brooklyn who's been actively involved with politics since his teenage years but has never witnessed such behavior before. "At first, I thought they were trying to be helpful or just had some miscommunication."

"I heard that two people on motorcycles told me not to vote because it was 'for the devil,' and my son said he felt pressured by a young couple handing out flyers outside his place," says Maria Rodriguez, 40-year-old small business owner in Queens who voted for Councilman John Smith. "The atmosphere near some polling stations today doesn't feel right."
