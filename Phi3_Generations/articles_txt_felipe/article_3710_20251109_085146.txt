Heavy snowfall in Alaska causes multiple accidents and power outages on Saturday morning  
March 5, 2024 - By Sarah Jennings for CNN

ANCHORAGE, AK - Multiple car accidents were reported across Anchorage due to heavy snow accumulation overnight. The National Weather Service issued a blizzard warning from Friday evening until Saturday morning in several areas of the city, leading authorities and residents alike into an unforeseen weather-induced chaos on their commute today.

The record-breaking 20 inches of snow blanketed major roadways early this weekend, causing hazardous driving conditions across Alaska'self winter wonderland.'According to Anchorage Police Department spokesperson Officer Mike Anderson,' we responded swiftly and deployed our officers on the ground. We understand these are tough circumstances for everyone involved but remain dedicated in keeping residents safe through this weather emergency.'

Several traffic collisions were reported, with authorities attributing most of them directly to poor road conditions resulting from Friday's snowfall,' said Anchorage Fire Department Captain Jane Roberts. 'We had multiple calls about stranded vehicles and individuals who required rescue assistance in treacherous driving situations.' One such accident led to a serious injury that necessitated airlifting the victim, underscoring how dangerous travel conditions can become during significant accumulation events like this one,' she added.

Another casualty of Friday's snowfall was Alaska Electric Light & Power Co., which reported thousands affected by power outages throughout their service territory across different parts of Anchorage and the surrounding areas, according to CEO Chris Thompson.'We are actively assessing damage in real-time but expect repair work on hundreds of poles alone. This is going to be a slow process,' said Thompson while stressing that restoration efforts were underway round-the-clock despite challenging weather conditions and high winds which could further delay power recovery operations.' 

Residents have been urged by local officials not to take unnecessary risks on the road. While some accidents cannot be prevented, authorities ask drivers to exercise caution when traveling or consider alternative transportation like public transit if they're able,' concluded Officer Anderson of Anchorage Police Department.'We understand that Alaskans pride themselves in their ability to endure and navigate through tough weather conditions. We appreciate the patience demonstrated by all during this time as we work together towards restoring normalcy.'

Several meteorologists confirmed these troubling forecasts on Friday evening, noting a potential blizzard with substantial snowfall possible across much of Anchorage and surrounding areas due to an approaching storm system. The National Weather Service warned residents that the city could see upwards 20 inches of accumulation before lifting by early Monday morning as winds picked up speed through the night, exacerbating driving hazards.'

Although most incidents occurred in heavily populated urban centers like downtown Anchorage and Midtown areas - which saw multiple accidents during Friday's blizzard conditions,' Alaska Governor Mike Dunleavy emphasized resilience as he visited the scene of an accident with other officials at I-5 overpass yesterday afternoon. 'We are all in this together, a community strong enough to rebound and recover from any situation life throws our way.' He added that safety was his top priority while stressing on proactive measures being taken by various state agencies
