According to preliminary results reported on Election Day afternoon after all precinct counts have been tallied up; Michael Rice leads with around 48% of total ballots cast while Democrat Thompson secures approximately 46%. Both candidates claim victory but it will take until Friday evening for the final official count - a process mandated by city law to ensure accuracy given such closely contested races.

The election took place across various boroughs, with voters casting their ballots at over 100 polling stations throughout New York City on Election Day itself and an additional three days of early voting that followed from October 25th through November 3rd for those unable or unwilling to vote in person.
