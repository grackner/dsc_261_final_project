On Saturday night in Houston, a group of high school seniors gathered at NRG Stadium to watch their beloved team take on its archrival for one final game this season – only for it all to end abruptly as the lights went out and chaos ensued amidst an unusually frigid December. The Texas High School Demons faced off against local powerhouse, Houston Thunderbolts in a nail-biter that would leave fans reeling but unharmed on this cold January evening when everything came to a sudden halt around 10:30 PM EST due to an unexpected blackout caused by the city's energy grid overloading.

"It was like someone had just flipped off all lights in one go," said Emily Gonzaleledescribing how darkness descended upon the crowd after hours of cheers and celebration following their victory on the field with a scoreline that would remain untarnished for historians: 35-28. "Nobody got hurt, but it was like being in an episode from 'Friday'."
The Demons had fought back against Thunderbolts all evening until their star quarterback fumbled the ball as he tried to run away from defenders and slip into a safe pocket for his final snap. His teammates chased after him, only to find themselves in darkness just when it seemed like this last game might not end on such an uncertain note – leaving some fans holding onto hope while others began looking at their calendars with trepidation as they realized that the season was officially over before its natural conclusion.
Jefferson High School Athletic Director, Mark Smith said: "We're grateful for everyone who showed up to cheer them on one last time but I guess things don't always go your way." The Texas Department of Education reported no injuries among players and attendees during this rare blackout; however there are concerns as power outages have become increasingly common in the city, which has struggled with energy capacity issues for years.
According to Mayor Anne Richardson's office: "We can confirm that our grid had been experiencing strains due to weather conditions and will continue working diligently on improvements." 
As power was being restored throughout the neighborhood of South Houston later in the night, local businesses are now feeling effects as well; one restaurant owner noted his establishment lost around $300 worth of perishable goods amidst hours-long waits for electricity to return.
