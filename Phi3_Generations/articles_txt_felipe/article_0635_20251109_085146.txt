Topic: Science - Climate

As temperatures continue to rise across Australia's Great Barrier Reef region, marine biologists have recorded unprecedented bleaching events that threaten this World Heritage site with possible ecological collapse by the end of the decade unless drastic action is taken (CNN). The Australian Institute for Marine Studies released a report last Thursday revealing record-breaking sea temperatures off Queensland and New South Wales, causing widespread coral bleaching.


According to Professor Linda Young from University of Sydney's School of Biological Sciences: "The reef has always been resilient but this is a climate change signal that the frequency and intensity are beyond anything we have seen in our lifetime." The Institute highlighted significant losses with over 20% of coral cover expected to perish within three months if current conditions persist.


In response, environmentalists rallying outside Parliament House today called for immediate policy action (DailyMail). "We cannot afford to wait another decade when we know what's happening here is a consequence of our own actions," urged Rachel Simmons from the Australian Conservation Foundation during the protest.


"Our children will see nothing but bleached ghost reef if we keep ignoring this wake-up call." The report underscored that over 90% of coral cover at many sites has already been lost, with a potential full recovery being highly unlikely without global efforts to mitigate climate change.
