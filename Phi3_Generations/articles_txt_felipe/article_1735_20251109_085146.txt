As the hurricane approached its landfall point around 5 AM local time this morning along Louisiana coastline with sustained wind speeds peaking between Category 3 and 4, citizens were rushed into preparations.

Residents were advised not only of the immediate threats but also of aftershocks and possible tornado activity accompanying this weather event, urging complete readiness for both scenarios as uncertainty lingered due to its strengthened intensity at such short notice."

Responders across affected communities worked around-the-clock under difficult conditions.
