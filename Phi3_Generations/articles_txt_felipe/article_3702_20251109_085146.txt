Topic: World - Middle East

Following an escalation in tensions between Israel and Hamas overnight on March 15th near Gaza City, more than a dozen rockets were fired into Israeli territory early this morning. The exchange of fire began shortly before dawn when armed forces clashed with militants along the border patrol areas within close proximity to each other; no casualties have been reported thus far as both sides remain on high alert after previous hostilities in December that left over 200 Palestinians dead and numerous Israeli settlers wounded.

The recent violence came amid a complex backdrop of regional instability, with the Persian Gulf witnessing an uptick in political unrest since January when oil prices surged by more than $3 per barrel globally because of sanctions imposed on Iran over its nuclear program and subsequent disputes regarding territorial boundaries.

Israeli Prime Minister Naftali Bennett issued a stern warning to Hamas leaders, stating "we will not tolerate any attempts at escalating this conflict outside the designated areas." He further urged his citizens residing in Jerusalem’s West Bank neighborhood of Sheikh Jarrah and occupied East Jerusalem'ty Mamilla Mall which has seen increased protest activity from Palestinian activists since November when Israel expanded settlement construction ahead of peace talks scheduled to begin next year.

In Gaza City, Hamas militant leader Yahya Sinwar denied any intention for conflict outside the territory asserting instead that "our struggle continues against oppression and injustice." He added: “We are prepared with all our weapons including rockets fired directly towards Israel when we see aggression.”

In response to increased hostilities, European Union High Representative Josep Borrell called on both parties not to escalate further stating "the risk of a wider conflict has never been greater." The spokesman for the United Nations secretary-general, Stephane Dujarric also warned that “The international community is deeply concerned about reports suggesting an increase in hostilities,” adding: "We urge all sides involved to avoid actions which can lead us into another intolerable crisis. Every life lost and every displaced person will forever remind of the futility of this violence."

Palestinian President Mahmoud Abbas condemned Israel's warning as “another escalation in a never-ending cycle” but also called on Hamas not to provoke further conflict saying: "We are calling for calm and dialogue between our Palestinian brothers, sisters living under the Israeli occupation. This is key if we want to maintain stability across Gaza."

As both sides continue their dispute over sovereignty in contested areas of Jerusalem’s Old City which has been at loggerhead since Israel captured West Bank during a 1967 war, international mediation efforts remain unsuccessful amid disagreements on how to achieve lasting peace. While the United Nations Security Council discussed condemning any incitement and violence between Palestinians in Gaza or Israeli settlers in occupied territories earlier this week; its final statement issued no tangible steps for resolving tensions, reflective of broader global impasse with little progress toward a two-state solution that was hoped to end decades of hostilities.
