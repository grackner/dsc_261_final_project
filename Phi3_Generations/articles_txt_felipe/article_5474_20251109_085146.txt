Bollywood Stalwart Amitabh Bachchan Announces Retirement from Acting: A Milestone in Indian Cinema History - January 30th, New Delhi to April 25th, Mumbai [Photo]  

By Shobhit Garg for cnnindia Updated at 18:45 IST Jan. 30, 2023, when the world-renowned Bollywood superstar Amitabh Bachchan announced his retirement from acting in Hindi films with immediate effect - January 30th will no longer be business as usual for one of India'theroads more iconic film personalities.  
Shubha Pande, CEO at FilmFest International said “Amitabh has been a defining force in Indian cinema; his retirement marks the end of an era.” The 78-year-old Bachchan was on stage during Mumbai’s prestigious annual film festival when he addressed hundreds who had come to pay tribute. "It is with utmost respect that I announce my decision not to act any further in Hindi films," the star said, prompting a standing ovation from his fans and fellow actors alike at what was supposed to be an evening of celebrating India’s rich cinematic legacy. He added: “I've cherished every role; it has been immensely gratifying but I feel that my physicality does not lend itself well for the demanding film industry anymore.” The veteran actor, who starred in over 100 movies since his debut back in ‘64 and bagged many National Film Awards throughout this career spans nearly six decades will continue to contribute by advising producers on movie screenplays.  
Reacting online, film lovers shared heartfelt messages saying "Amitabh has always been a symbol of strength," while others reflected: “We’ve all grown up watching Amitabh in his myriad characters; it feels surreal,” wrote Anjali Deshpande.  
Industry veterans like director Sanjay Leela Bhansali and actress Katrina Kaif expressed shock, with the latter saying "I am deeply saddened to hear about Amitabh’s decision but truly respect him for his courage in choosing what suits best for himself.” The announcement has sent ripples across Indian film industry; however, they are mixed. Actors feel a sense of loss as Bachchan's work became synonymous with the very notion of acting itself—an emotion echoed by several fans who shared their reactions on Twitter: "I felt nostalgic while watching his speech but also proud to see him honoring himself," wrote Sunil Kumar, 27.

Bachchan's departure from Indian cinema comes at a time when streaming platforms are taking over traditional theatrical releases with original content in Bollywood; however, this announcement only highlighted Bachchan’s unique position as an actor who never fit into any mold of 'typical' superstar image.  
Bachchan had notably been one among few actors to grace silver screens alongside legends like Raj Kapoor and Dilip Kumar in the ’60s; however, his unmatched ability to play a myriad roles – from heroic protagonists to anti-heroes - coupled with distinctive screen presence is what set him apart.
