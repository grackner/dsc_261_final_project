Topic: Health - Sleep

A study conducted by renowned sleep specialists at John Hopkins University has revealed alarming statistics on American adult's sleep habits as of February 2023: Only a quarter get the recommended seven to nine hours nightly, while nearly half suffer from insufficient rest. Experts link poor sleep with negative health outcomes like heart disease and diabetes.

In New York City alone, thousands report chronic lack of shut-eye on any given night due to various stressors in their lives. Dr. Emily Carter, lead researcher at the Johns Hopkins Sleep Disorders Center emphasizes that "adequate sleep is fundamental for physical and mental health." 

The study found factors like excessive screen time before bed and consuming caffeine late can disrupt natural circadian rhythms. Dr. Carter suggests establishing a relaxing pre-sleep routine to mitigate these issues, advising people "to turn off electronic devices at least an hour prior."
