Topic: Politics - Elections

Democratic primaries in swing states across America reached a fever pitch today as thousands flocked to early voting locations and mail-in ballots poured into drop boxes ahead of Election Day on November 3, reports CNN National Desk with Associated Press contributors. As we approach the critical juncture for choosing our nation's leader in just over two weeks, pundits across political spectrum have offered differing analyses as to which candidate stands a better chance at securing the Democratic nomination and eventually taking on President Trump in November elections that could shape American politics profoundly.

In Wisconsin where early voting concluded today with 15% of voters having already cast their ballots, Gov. Tony Evers urged citizens to keep lines moving smoothly while ensidering the challenges posed by COVID-19 pandemic: "I want everyone who is eligible and wants to vote on Tuesday come out in person – or as soon as they can."

In contrast, New York City Mayor Bill de Blasio urged constituents yesterday through a series of town halls that voting by mail would be their preferred method: "Whether you're sick with the virus yourself, worried about exposing someone else to it...mail-in votes are safe and secure."

As for experts on voter turnout patterns in swing states like Michigan, Ohio, and Florida where Election Day is tomorrow - these haven’t been kind: "Given current trends, we could see record lows or highs this election year. It's unpredictable," says Prof. Elizabeth Warren of Harvard University Political Science department who closely tracks national polls & voting behavior data for her research on democratic participation in the US.

Turnout estimates have been hard to come by, but with 26 states reporting partial results and Ohio already over halfway through its count (47%), there are signs that this year could see record levels of early voters as well according Prof. John Adams an esteemed Political Analyst at Columbia University's School of Journalism who has spent his career analyzing elections since the 1960 race between Kennedy and Nixon: "The last two election cycles have seen surges in mail-in voting, especially among Democrats - I wouldn't be surprised if this year is just as busy."
