In a significant move towards bolstering its national security measures, Australia announced on Monday plans to construct an underwater surveillenzone extending into international waters near its northern coastline. The proposed zone will span approximately 200 square miles and is expected to become operational by the end of next year following rigorous testing phases aimed at detecting foreign submarines attempting unauthorized intrusion in Australian Exclusive Economic Zone (EEZ).

Defense Minister Peter Dutton revealed, "The establishment of this underwater surveillance zone is a critical step towards safeguarding our nation and interests. We will not stand idle while threats to peaceful nations lurk beneath the waves." The announcement comes amidst escalating tensions in regional waters as several countries grapple with increasing submarine activities near territorial borders, which experts warn could potentially compromise national security if left unchecked.

Accordingly, Australia's Department of Defense commissioned a study by internationally-respected marine science research institution Oceanic Research Group (ORG), asserting the need for enhanced surveillance mechanisms to monitor submarine traffic effectively and swiftly respond should any unauthorized presence be detected. The ORG's report, which has since been endorsed by multiple regional allies sharing similar security concerns over maritime activities in their EEZ territories as well, states the construction of an underwater surveillance zone would provide Australia with a strategic advantage to monitor submarine movements across international waters.
