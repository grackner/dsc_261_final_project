Topic: Business - Markets Now

On Thursday morning at exactly 7:30 a.m., stock markets worldwide were shaken by unprecedented volatility as technology shares took hits across major exchanges in Asia and Europe, setting the tone for Wall Street'treaction throughout the day.[1] Trading commenced on NSE India (National Stock Exchange of India), BSE Mumbai, Kolkata and Delhi bourses from 9:30 a.m., with Hindustan Times reporting that investors were already feeling anxious as early figures came in showing sharp declines among top tech companies.[2]

In Tokyo's benchmark Nikkei index fell by over 1%, while the S&P/ASX 200 (Australian share market) dropped more than half a percent and Frankfurt’s DAX saw losses of nearly two-and-a-half points as these markets struggled to deal with tech shares hitting record high prices for months.[3]

A leading Wall Street analyst, Jonathan Smithson at Goldman Sachs told CNN Business "The sudden decline in technology stock valuations suggests investors are reassessing the market's sustainability and question whether growth expectations have been overly optimistic."[4] He added that such shifts can lead to greater opportunities for well-positioned investors, saying: “This is often a time when selective buying becomes lucrative. We might see some smart picking of quality tech firms here.”

The dip in the markets came after several large technology companies announced strong earnings results and raised their forecasts for future profits.[5] For example, Apple Inc., reported an increase in quarterly revenue by 12% to $90.1 billion with a net income of $23.74 per share – both exceeding analyst expectations but failing against the recent soaring tech sector valuation and hype.[6]

Meanwhile, CNBC interviewed an experienced investor named Margaret Thompson who has been closely monitoring technology stocks for over a decade and commented that "it’s not unusual to see such fluctuations. What we are witnessing is normal market ebb-and-flow."[7] Despite this viewpoint, the mood amongst everyday traders remained cautious as they watched tech shares wobble across markets worldwide on Thursday morning.[8]

The situation sparked concerns over a potential shift in investor sentiment and market dynamics with experts speculating whether recent years' growth might be slowing down or if we are merely seeing temporary pullbacks. The UBS Global Wealth Management division released an official statement, noting that: "Our analysis suggests the current downturn is due to a rapid reassessment of expectations following several months in which technology stock valuations reached all-time highs."[9]

As markets closed out their Thursday activities by midnight ET across Europe and Wall Street, there was an evident sense of anticipation for what the rest of Friday might bring. Experts at JP Morgan suggest that "if current trends continue overtaking into next week with some sectors possibly seeing a recovery in tech stocks as investors recalibrate."[10]
