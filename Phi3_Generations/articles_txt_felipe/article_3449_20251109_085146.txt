Topic: Health - Sleep

A local school in Springfield has reported an unprecedented number of absences due to poor sleep among students and staff alike over the past month. The phenomenon began drawing attention at Franklin Elementary School when attendance rates plummeted by a staggering 30% compared to this time last year, with most reports tracing back issues surrounding insufficient nighttime rest as potential causes (Springfield Health Department).

Dr. Emily Stanton, the school's in-house health consultant confirmed that "Many of our students and staff have come forward expressing difficulty falling asleep or staying asleep." She added: "The disruption to their routine appears linked with increased exposure to screens before bedtime among adolescents - a pattern seen nationwide (American Psychological Association)."

A recent study conducted by local sleep experts suggests that nearly 65% of the students and about half of all staff report an increase in night-time anxiety, potentially due to ongoing academic pressures or familial stressors.
