In terms of percentages, teenagers showed a minimal difference while young adults saw an increase in heart-related complications by nearly five per cent compared with those getting at least seven hours sleep nightly.

The next steps include more extensive, global studies with larger diverse populations across different continents aiming at getting a clearer picture of how sleep deprivation affects heart health over time and identifying strategies to improve our nightly hours which would be essential in combating growing cardiovascular diseases worldwide.
