EU Commission Reveals Plans for Digital Market Overhaul Amid Growing Concerns of Monopoly Power in Silicon Valley's Tech Giants - September 27, 2tabled at the European Council headquarters by officials today is likely to set off a chain reaction across continents as American tech behemoth Facebook and Alphabet-owned Google will not escape scrutiny.

Brussels has become increasingly worried about growing market dominance of its biggest companies, particularly in digital services like social media, advertising, cloud computing, AI development and the Internet itself that have a significant global reach. While tech firms argue their expansion is necessary for economic growth and job creation, critics say they are stifling competition at the expense of innovation elsewhere.

The EU Commission's plan proposes measures to regulate big tech companies more closely by enhancing transparency around how algorithms prioritize content online; establishing rules on data portability - allowing consumers to take their personal information with them when they switch services, which will put pressure on the likes of Facebook and Google. EU competition chief Margrethe Vestager insisted that "our goal is not only fairness but also maintain an innovative ecosystem."

"We aim for a more level playing field where startups can thrive without fearing their every move being replicated by these tech giants, and consumers get better options," she said in Brussels yesterday. "No one wants to live under the thumb of Big Tech monopolies." Vestager's statements came ahead of a closed-door meeting with industry experts this weekend where EU commissioner Thierry Breton will discuss details and expectations from tech companies regarding how they can adapt their practices within these new regulations.

The move has already sparked concerns among some European startups, fearing that tough rules may discourage foreign investment or hurt established firms' ability to compete effectively against Silicon Valley giants in the EU market; several representatives of emerging tech companies from across Europe shared their worries with industry journalists yesterday.

A key part of this plan will see EU creating an executive body known as the Digital Competition Council (DCC) which would enforce data portability rules across all sectors and monitor algorithm fairness to prevent misuse or biases affecting user experience online; officials hope DCC can effectively curtail unfair practices by tech giants that currently dominate markets with their enormous resources, algorithms powered through big-scale AI development.
