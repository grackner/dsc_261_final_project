Topic: Business - After-Hours

An explosion rocked a downtown office building in New York City late Tuesday evening, causing partial evacuation and extensive property damage but fortunately sparing any injuries to individuals inside the workplace at Broad Avenue Tower during business hours. According to eyewitness reports from employees still on-site post-evacuation order as of 8 p.m., a series of small charges triggered by an unknown source detonated in separate offices within the structure, located directly across Union Square Park near major financial districts.

Security personnel and emergency responders are currently investigating after several blasts were reported around 7:45 pm local time that have since been contained following multiple explosions throughout Tuesday evening into early Wednesday morning. The office building is owned by conglomerate corporation Global Dynamics, with operations spanning financial services and international trade across the tri-state area.

Jennifer Smythe, a security manager present during the incident at 7:30 pm who now serves as first responder team lead onsite said, "We immediately went into lockdown protocols when we heard commotion outside our doors; everyone was instructed to shelter in place." A few blocks away inside Union Square Park's cafe and ice cream shop owners reported unusual noises just before 7:45 pm but nothing that looked or sounded like an explosion.
