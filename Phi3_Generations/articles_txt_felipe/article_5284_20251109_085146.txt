Topic: Business - Markets Now

A small-town retailer has managed to tap into a national market trend by selling its organic produce directly online from California's Central Valley during peak times when fresh goods are scarce across the nation, reports CNN Business and DailyMail on April 14th. Started in early spring as demand for farm-fresh vegetables surged due to supply chain disruinations caused by the pandemic, 'Valley Greens Market' has quickly become a sensation among health enthusiasts across multiple states who flock online during regular hours when local stores are closed or facing limitations.

"Our business skyrocketed overnight," says Elena Ramirez, founder of Valley Greens Market from her family’s farm in Kingsburg, California's heartland about 25 miles southwest of Fresno - a hub for organic farming and agricultural innovation. "Customers come not just during peak hours but late at night too when they can access our fresh offerings that others don't have."

Ramirez’ business model bypasses traditional retail, relying solely on an efficient online ordering system to directly connect with customers nationwide via third-party delivery services. This comes as the global crisis continues to disrupt typical consumer habits and market operations — making Ramirez' venture a surprising success story that has even attracted national attention from large retailers considering expansion in the organic produce space, CNN reports on April 15th citing interviews with industry analysts.

"The growth of businesses like Valley Greens Market is an indicator for big-box stores to adapt quickly," says Alex Chen, a supply chain expert at University College London who studies retail trends and food accessibility across nations in the aftermath of global disruptions caused by events such as pandemics. "There’s no turning back on this — these businesses have opened new paths that were previously unthinkable."

Ramirez' venture capitalized on a significant national shift towards healthier eating, fueled further by the need to cut down unnecessary trips in an era of physical distancing. The Central Valley is home to over 30 organic farms and has been supplying fresh produce for decades — its proximity and reputation made it ideal from which Ramirez could source high-quality vegetables year round, CNN Business reports on April 16th citing interviews with local growers.

"The Valley Greens Market's growth is a reflection of consumers prioritizing their health amidst the pandemic," says Dr. James Patterson, an epidemiologist at UCLA who studies consumer behavior during crises and social impact on businesses in California specifically since January 2019 when he began his research project which has been published extensively across academic journals — including The Lancet last month for the first time looking into this phenomenon. "Consumers are demanding more transparency, authenticity and direct access to their food sources."

Ramirez aims not just to capitalize on current trends but also promote sustainable agricultural practices by educating customers through her online platform about where the produce they buy comes from.
