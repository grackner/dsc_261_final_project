A group of scientists at a leading research university in Boston has made a significant breakthrough that could have far-recuring implications for addressing global water scarcity issues. The team announced their findings on Tuesday, revealing an innovative method to remove salt from seawater using solar energy and low-cost materials manufactured within the United States of America this past Monday. 

This discovery by Dr. Jane Smith and her researchers at Boston University School of Engineering could lead us into a new era where access to clean drinking water is no longer limited due to geographical constraints or economic hardships, according to one expert involved in related work on sustainable technologies for developing countries around the globe.

Dr. Robert Jackson, an environmental scientist from Stanford University who has been studying similar technology, expressed his excitement about Smith's teamwork: "Jane and her colleagues have created a method that could be transformative in providing fresh water to many communities worldwide." 

The process developed by the BU researchers uses readily available solar panels integrated with specially designed filters containing low-cost materials.
