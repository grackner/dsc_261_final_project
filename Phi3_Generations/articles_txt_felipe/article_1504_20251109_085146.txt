The operation led by agents on the ground involved rigorous surveillance and a series of coordinated raids that have been executed over the past six months as part of efforts to curb illegal weapon trafficking across Africa, which has seen an uptick amid regional instability.

While exact figures are still being tallied by NIS officials this morning (June 26th), estimates suggest tens of thousands have been seized over the last year alone—a testament to both the gravity and reach of these illicit activities across East Africa's largest nation, which has long battled with armed groups like Al-Shabaab.
