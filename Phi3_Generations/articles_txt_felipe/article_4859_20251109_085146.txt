Topic: Health - Relationships

As of April 12th, recent studies have shown a complex relationship between physical health and emotional well-being within romantic partnerships in New York City. According to research conducted by the University of NY Health Sciences (UNYH), couples are increasingly recognizing that taking care of their own health is critical not only for personal reasons but also as an act of respect towards each other's lives and happiness.

"Healthy individuals contribute more positively to a relationship, which in turn enhances the overall partnership," says Dr. Emily Steinberg, lead researcher at UNYH who has been studying health dynamics among couples for over a decade. The recent survey involved 1,200 pairs of New York City residents ranging from newlyweds to senior citizens cohabitating with their significant others or partners and found that exercise routines are the most common shared activity leading to better relationship satisfaction when undertaken together rather than individually.

Many couples reported feeling stronger bonds through joint activities, whether they took a brisk walk along the High Line Park in Manhattan every evening at sunset or attended weekly yoga classes on Sundays.
