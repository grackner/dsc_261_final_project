Feb 21: A traveler from Boston who embarked on a solo journey across Southeast Asia has sparked controversy over his extended stay in various countries amidst the global pandemic and subsequent criticism for not returning home sooner, reports CNN. The Massachusetts resident's spontaneous trip began last September as many Americans sought to escape lockdown fatigue by hopping international flights with little notice or official travel permissions.


The 31-year-curious voyager initially planned a three-week visit to Thailand before venturing further into the continent, but his journey has now spanned over six months and includes stops in countries like Vietnam, Cambodia, Laos, and Myanmar. His travels have caught attention due both to their audacity given the pandemic circumstances and because he hasn't returned to American shores as planned back on November 30th when restrictions lifted slightly for U.S. citizens arriving home through special programs like USAAEO-M1, where vaccination status was a prerequisite, CNN sources confirmed.


"Travel has always been my passion and freedom of movement is something I value greatly," says the traveler in an interview with journalists on November 4th when his journey began before he entered Thailand's dense jungle region for exploration, which may have delayed his departure from local tourism.
