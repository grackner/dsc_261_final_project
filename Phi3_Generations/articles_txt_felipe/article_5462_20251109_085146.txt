Topic: Business - After-Hours

Boston businesses report record evening sales in wake of pandemic  

Massachusetts – A group of local entrepreneurs is basking in success as the city's restaurant and retail sectors reach unprecedented levels, with after-hours earnings skyrocketing this past week. 

The surge comes just months into a challenging year that saw businesses grappling with lockdown restrictions before gradually opening up again to revitalize the economy in Greater Boston and beyond.  

"After five long years of struggle, we're finally seeing some sunlight on our faces," said Maria Gonzalez-Vargas, owner of La Dolce Vita Italian restaurant, which has been operating at a 30% capacity due to social distancing protocols throughout the pandemic.  

On Friday night alone, her establishment sold out all inventory within hours after implementing new safety measures and reserving tables for larger parties without advance bookings — strategies that have worked wonders as patrons eagerly return to normalcy amid continued caution with COVID-19 restrictions still in place across many regions.  

City Mayor Thomas McDermott, Jr., acknowledging the positive turnaround during his weekend visit alongside local officials and business owners at various establishments around Faneuil Hall on Saturday evening (October 28), remarked: "It's incredibly gratifying to see Boston come alive again after these trying times. We must remain vigilant but also celebrate the resilience of our city."  

Several other businesses, from cozy cafés like Brew & Beans with its 20-person capacity rule now in effect thanks to safety improvements since early September when only takeout options were available — down by a full half compared to typical pre-pandemic days at times during weekends and holidays.  

Local bookstore Olive Branch Books reports over $15,000 in after-hours sales this past month with customers enjoying the comfortable atmosphere created through plexiglass dividers between tables where they browse shelves packed full of classic literature alongside modern fiction — an arrangement that has brought back a sense of normalcy to what had become strictly workplace environments.  

Café Manager Sam Chang explains, "We've managed by providing excellent service while keeping our customers safe and comfortable in their own homes for most part." 

In retail spaces such as the upscale Boston Fashion District store located on Newbury Street near Harvard Square — which recently introduced personalized styling appointments wherein clients can meet with fashion consultants after hours, transforming its typically quiet weekday setting into a bustling hotspot.  

Store Director Emily Patel notes that this pivot has not only revitalized her sales but also brought back foot traffic to the area which was once one of Boston's most vibrant and dynamic shopping destinations, especially before COVID-19 hit: "We are thrilled with our progress so far — it feels like we're getting used to a new normal again."  

While some sectors still face headwinds due to varying regional restrictions across states that have not caught up on reopening timelines, businesses continue adapt and thrive within the boundaries of caution by offering unique experiences while keeping customers safe — as one local waitress shared: "It's amazing how everyone is supporting each other.
