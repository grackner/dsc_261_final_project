In a daring move that underscts escalating tensions in East Asian waters, North Korea launched an uncrewed submarine test-firing its latest Sejong the Great (DPRK's largest underwater vehicle) on Tuesday near Pyongyang. Authorities believe this demonstration is a direct challenge to international maritime norms and could signify preparations for more aggressive tactics in territorial disputes within contested waters, particularly around key chokepoints like the South China Sea.

The test-firing of an uncrewed submarine occurred at approximately 10:35 AM local time (7:35 PM UTC) off Pyongyang's coast and was captured on surveillance footage that has since been aired by state media outlets, drawing immediate condemnation from the international community.

A South Korean official stated, "This act is reckless and could destabilize an already fragile peace in this region." Meanwhile, Japan's Ministry of Defense issued a statement emphasizing their commitment to maintaining regional security: "The Japanese government strictly prohibits any military activity that disrupts the safety and tranquility of our waters," adding it will continue working closely with allies.
