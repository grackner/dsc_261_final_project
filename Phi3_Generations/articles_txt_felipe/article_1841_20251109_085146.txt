Topic: Style - Beauty

A renowned makeup artist in New York City has developed an innovative skincare routine specifically designed for people with oily and acne-prone skin types who also aspire to look glamorous without compromising on style. Jessica Clarke, known for her work as a beauty consultant at high-end fashion events across the city since 2010, has recently revealed what she calls "The Elite Cleanse."


Clarke'termarketed program began last month and comes amidst an ongoing skin health trend focused heavily towards cleanse products that don’t strip natural oils. Clarke explained her routine as being “a combination of essential oil treatments, acne-fighting masks, lightweight foundations with SPF protection, setting spray, and mineral blushes.”


The 29-year-old beauty consultant developed the program through extensive research into both skincare science and makeup artistry. Her innovative approach aims to cater specifically for those who find their oily skin or acne an obstacle in achieving glamorous looks commonly showcased on runways by celebrities with perfect complexions. Clarke was inspired after working at the Met Gala three years ago, where she observed firsthand how makeup can transform even challenging textures to enhance beauty without using harsh chemicals that may cause further skin damage or breakouts.


"Oily and acne-prone skin is often seen as a barrier in achieving glamourous looks but I believe it's our responsibility, especially within the beauty industry, not only for ourselves to overcome these challenges,” Clarke stated at her recently held workshop. She elaborated that "the key lies with selecting products wisely and maintaining consistent skincare habits."


Clarke has received positive feedback from several followers of hers who have tried the program, including a regular client named Maria Thompson: “I’ve always struggled to look like myself due to my oily skin. Ever since I started using Clarke's Elite Cleanse, not only do I feel confident but also radiant and glowy.”


While there are no official studies yet on the efficacy of Clarke's unique skincare routine in significantly improving acne or reducing oil production over time, it’s clear that she has struck a chord with those who share her struggles. As more individuals learn about this innovative approach to combatting oily skin while still aiming for glamourous looks, the demand is likely only set to increase amongst skincare-conscious New Yorkers and beyond in other metropolitan areas worldwide as Clarke plans on expanding “The Elite Cleanse” program across major cities.


Next steps include partnering with leading cosmetic brands for product endorsements while continuously improving the regimen based on user feedback, making it more accessible and practical to follow every day of their busy lives without having to compromise between maintaining a clear complexion and looking glamorous.
