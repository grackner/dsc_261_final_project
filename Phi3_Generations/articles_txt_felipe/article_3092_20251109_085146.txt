Topic: Business - Media

In a landmark development for media convergence in the Asia-Pacific region, Nanyang Polytechnic of Singapore announced on Monday that they will launch an online business school aimed at equipping students with skills to navigate digital transformation within various industries by mid-2025. The initiative comes amid growing concerns over traditional educational pathways becoming increasingly irrelevant in a rapidly evolving tech landscape, and is set to be spearheaded by industry veterans from Silicon Valley's top echelons who will serve as part of the faculty.

Dr. Lim Boon Eng, Deputy Vice-Chancellor (Research) at Nanyang Polytechnic stated in a press conference on Monday: "We are thrilled to announce this innovative education program that draws upon years of experience from our industry veterans and experts." The new online school will offer courses covering digital marketing, data analytics, coding languages, cybersecurity measures among others. As per the plan, a specialized team led by seasoned Silicon Valley professionals such as former Google Chief Technology Officer Scott Confrey is set to take charge of curriculum development and delivery modeled on best practices within leading tech companies in California and beyond Asia Pacific.

Nanyang Polytechnic'thas been known for its forward-looking approach towards education, pioneering the use of technology since 1984 as a way not just to streamline administrative tasks but also improve student outcomes through personalized learning plans powered by data analytics. The new initiative has sparked interest among media companies across Asia Pacific which have been grappling with how best to train their workforce for digital disruption and automation that threatens traditional roles within the industry, leading them towards a broader understanding of technology trends in business operations management (BOM).

Accordingly, representatives from major news corporations including Reuters Group Pte Ltd., Bloomberg Southeast Asia Limited and South China Morning Post have already expressed interest to send their employees for the program. They see this as a timely solution towards preparing personnel in age-old journalism practices while embracing emerging tools like Artificial Intelligence (AI) that are expected to reshape how newsrooms operate within five years’ time, said industry veteran Aisha Lee of Reuters Group Pte Ltd: "The digital media landscape is constantly evolving. As part of our commitment towards future-proofing ourselves and being competitive in this changing business environment, we see the immense value that Nanyang Polytechnic brings to their online Business School initiative."

As companies continue navigating uncharted waters amid disruption by digital media on traditional news outlets worldwide – a phenomenon widely known as 'fake news' - industry leaders are urged not just to adapt but thrive. The launch of Nanyang Polytechnic’s online Business School is seen as an important first step towards equipping future business leaders with the necessary skills and knowledge they need in order to navigate digital media transformation while maintaining journalistic integrity amidst growing skepticism from audiences worldwide regarding traditional news sources. 

A press release sent out by Nanyang Polytechnic following Monday's announcement highlighted that this new initiative not only addresses the immediate needs of industries facing disruption due to digital media, but also seeks solutions for long-term sustainability across sectors undergoing transformations within Asia Pacific.
