An early-morning match between Liverpool and Chelsea on Saturday at Anfield saw a surprising scoreline with an unlikely win for Liverpool by three goals to one against the reigning champions in Merseyside, England - February 14th. The unexpected turn of events had both sides scrambling as they digested their results while fans erupted into cheers and applause throughout Old Trafford stadium during halftime celebrations for Liverpool's star player Mohamed Salah who scored twice in the first half, along with Sadio Mane contributing a memorable goal.


In an exclusive interview after his performance, Salah stated: "This is such an honor to represent my hometown and win against Chelsea at home ground," reflecting on Liverpool's 3-1 victory in the Premier League match that ended with overwhthrilling supporters rushing onto the field.

Liverpool manager Jurgen Klopp, who watched from afar but was reportedly ecstatic about his team's win said: "We came here as underdogs and we left as winners. This victory will motivate us even more going forward." Chelsea coach Maurizio Sarri offered congratulations to Liverpool saying it showed the importance of belief, unity on a team when facing off against top teams like these two clubs do regularly in the Premier League: "Liverpool did not come out this match easily. They are determined winners and we have seen that tonight."

As for Mane's contribution to Liverpool winning their first home game of the season, he described his feeling on what led him towards scoring saying,"Every toucher I make here has an extra meaning because it’s my hometown. That energy feeds into me and helps push me onto successes like today."

Reacting live from outside Chelsea's Stamford Bridge, supporter James Brown said: "I never thought we would see Liverpool winning against them here in Merseyside before the season even started. It feels amazing to witness such a historic moment for our city!" The game was watched by millions of viewers nationwide who were glued onto their television screens as every second passed with high tension and excitement, especially during Salah's goalscoring which left fans at Stamford Bridge in stunned silence.

With this incredible win against Chelsea on the home front for Liverpool supporters it seems that an early start could be hereafter seen throughout seasons as they enter 2019 with a newfound sense of confidence and belief among their ranks, while still remaining humble enough to continue working hard at training camps.
