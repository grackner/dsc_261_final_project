Topic: World - China

China's capital city experienced unprecedented levels of air pollution yesterday as fine particulate matter reached a hazardous density across Beijing and its surrounding regions, with the national environmental monitoring system recording PM2.5 concentrations up to 18 times higher than World Health Organization (WHO) guidelines for safe exposure limits by mid-afternoon local time on March 30th, according to official reports from China's State Environmental Protection Administration and city pollution control data released today in the region.


"The current situation is dire; we are seeing air quality deteriorate at alarming rates," said Dr. Meng Weiwei of Beijing University's School of Public Health during a press conference held this afternoon, reporting that PM2.5 particulates were particularly high in residential areas and near industrial sites around the city perimeter due to vehicle emissions combined with ongoing construction work following recent heavy rains.


The Chinese government has deployed thousands of its own troops alongside fire services across Beijing's urban landscape, using water trucks and hoses to douse visible smoke plumes rising from power plants and industrial facilities that are unable to cease operations due to contractual obligations or economic pressures. Despite these efforts, the density has yet to decrease significantly since morning rush hour began last Wednesday, with many citizens expressing fear for their health as a result of continued exposure on social media platforms such as Weibo and Douyin (Tencent's local version).
