Topic: Health - Sleep

In a small town in Maine on Tuesday night, local authorities received reports of strange sleep-related events affecting numerous residents. Over two hundred people have been brought to the hospital with symptoms ranging from extreme drowsiness and memory loss to paralysis while asleep (1). The episodes began last weekend during a full moon, according to initial observations by Dr. Emily Stanton, an expert in sleep studies based at Dartmouth-Hitchcock Medical Center (2).

Dr. Stanton was called out of her regular duties when the cases started piling up and she quickly noticed that these were no ordinary disturbande; they seem to have a temporal pattern linked with lunar phases. "The prevalence coincides quite closely with specific times during our full moon, which suggests there might be an external environmental factor at play here," Stanton says (3).

Authorities are urging people not to take any risks and advised against going out after dusk when the problematic sleep episodes typically occur. "We understand this situation is unsettling for everyone in town, but our top priority remains safety; we need individuals home during these periods," says Chief of Police Thomas Harris (4).

More than a dozen eyewitnesses have shared their experiences on social media platforms and while some claim to be frightened by what they've seen others speculate about its cause. "I thought it was just bad dreams at first, but then I woke up in the living room with my wife beside me; we both felt disoriented," shares resident Mark Simmons (5). 

Scientists are still investigating what's causing these peculiar sleeping incidents as more reports come pouring in from surrounding towns and cities. Meanwhile, local hospital administrators have requested additional staff to manage the influx of patients arriving with unusual symptoms directly related to their slumber (6). This issue may be a wake-up call for communities nationwide about taking proper precautions during sleep; experts warn that while we often attribute insomnia or disturbances in our rest solely to personal stressors, environmental factors can also come into play.
