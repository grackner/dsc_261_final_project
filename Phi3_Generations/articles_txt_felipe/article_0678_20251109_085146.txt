Topic: Science - Weather

A powerful storm system is expected to hit New York City this weekend with forecasts predicting wind speeds up to hurricane force and heavy rainfall leading to potential flash flooding in parts of the metropolitan area, according to officials from The Weather Channel on Friday. Meteorologists have warned residents that they should prepare for severe weather conditions beginning Saturday afternoon until Monday night as a high-pressure system moves southward across several northeastern states before turning east and then north towards New York City by late Sunday evening or early Monday morning, bringing with it the risk of flooding.

"This is not just another snowstorm," said meteorologist Dr. Samantha Lee at The Weather Channel's office in Atlanta on Friday afternoon as she reviewed data from her colleagues across multiple states working to monitor and predict storm movements for New York City residents, who could face potentially life-threatening weather conditions by the weekend’s end. "It will bring significant flooding risks with heavy rain accompanied by powerful wind gusts that are expected to reach hurricane force in some areas," she added while flipping through pages of forecast maps and computer simulations showing possible scenarios for New York City based on weather data coming from various sources nationwide.

Emergency Management Director John Carter announced a state-of-emergency declaration earlier today, allowing local officials to access additional resources as they prepare evacuation plans in response to these anticipated extreme events across the five boroughs of NYC where he oversees disaster response and recovery operations for millions living within 10 miles from city hall. Carter stated during a press conference on Friday morning that his team has been working closely with state agencies such as FEMA, National Weather Service, US Army Corps Of Engineers among others in preparing contingencies should conditions deteriorate beyond their control:

"We are monitoring this situation very carefully and have issued several warnings urging citizens to stay off the streets when possible during these dangerous times," Carter said after reviewing storm updates from The Weather Channel. "There will be an enormous strain on our infrastructure given how dense New York City is; we must prepare for potential power outages, water disruption along with transportation delays."

City Council Member Maria Rodriguez echoed similar sentiments regarding the urgency of this situation while also providing insight into what she believes are some key steps city dwellers can take in preparing themselves and their homes prior to Sunday afternoon when conditions begin worsening rapidly. "Residents should secure loose items outside such as furniture, plan for alternative shelter options like hotels or friends' houses if they don’t feel safe staying at home during the storm," said Rodriguez after visiting with representatives from city emergency management teams on Thursday afternoon while touring disaster response centers around Queens and Brooklyn.

New York City Mayor Bill de Blasio issued a statement last night confirming that New York State’s Emergency Operations Center (EOC) is fully activated as part of efforts coordinating regional resources in preparation for the storm system expected to hit NYC beginning Saturday afternoon through Monday evening, which he described has been rated by The Weather Channel as being among some "the most powerful and potentially dangerous weather systems we've seen this year."
