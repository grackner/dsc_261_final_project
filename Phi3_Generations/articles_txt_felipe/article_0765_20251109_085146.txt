A devastating earthquake measuring a magnitude of 7.8 struck off the coastal city of Kyoto in Japan early this morning local time [DATE]. The tremors were felt across much of central Japan, prompting immediate responses from authorities and relief organizations as they work to assist those affected by this natural disaster on July 14th.[JAPAN]

According to initial reports issued shortly after the quake struck at around 5:30 a.m., local time (7:30 UTC), it jolted people into action, with hundreds already evacuated from coastal areas in Kyoto that were deemed vulnerable to tsunamis sparked by such an earthquake[JAPAN]

"The city has gone into a state of emergency," said Hiroshi Watanabe, spokesperson for the local government. "Our top priority is ensuring public safety." He went on to add that around 200 people were reported safe in evacuation centers and rescue crews have begun searching through areas still partially enveloped by early morning fog[JAPAN]

Early into the disaster, Kyoto National Museum's head curator expressed concerns about potential damage to historical artworks within. "We are taking immediate precautions," said Kenji Suemura. He mentioned that all items have been checked for stability and there were no serious damages reported[JAPAN]

An initial count of the quake-affected population suggests upwards of 30,000 individuals could be impacted by aftershocks or direct consequences in central Kyoto. Experts warn that tremors are likely to continue for days and potential tsunamis should not be discounted[JAPAN]

Kyoto Mayor Hiroshi Akutsu emphasized the importance of staying calm, adding: "We've dealt with earthquakes before. Our focus is on keeping everyone safe." He also announced an immediate meeting between local and national authorities to strategize long-term relief efforts[JAPAN]

The Japan Meteorological Agency has warned that aftershocks are expected for the next two weeks, while global seismologists advise remaining vigilant. Experts stress there is no reason at this moment to expect a tsunami in Kyoto's vicinity but warn it could not be ruled out completely until more information about undersea movements becomes available[JAPAN]

Local businesses have closed their doors, and several train services halted as they comply with government advisories. In the aftermath of such a catastrophic event in Japan's historically earthquake-prone regions can be disconcerting for many citizens. The city administration has assured that financial aid would come from national reserves to ensure immediate relief efforts[JAPAN]

As rescue operations kick into high gear, the international community stands ready to provide assistance if necessary with multiple nations' embassies expressing condolences and offering support in their statements on social media.[/TEXT]
