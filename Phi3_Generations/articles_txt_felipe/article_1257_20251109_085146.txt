A local family in Charleston has started a new seafood restaurant that is quickly turning heads with its innovative menu and sustainable practices. "Oyster Oasis" opened last week at the popular Seafare Market on Easton Street, offering an assortment of oysters from around South Carolina's coastline as well as creative dishes utilizing other local seafood. The restaurant features a small seating area with panoramic views over Charleston harbor and boasts eco-friendly practices like composting food waste on-site, according to the owner Samantha Hayes.

"As someone who has always loved fresh seafood from our local waters here in South Carolina," says Mrs. Hayes of her motivation for opening Oyster Oasis, "I wanted to bring this joy and experience it with others while also doing my part to support sustainable fishing practices." Her menu includes dishes like oyster ceviche with seasonal heirloom tomatoes (which will soon be in-season again), roasted sea bass served on a bed of microgreens, and sweet potato gnocchi sautéed with calamari rings.

Customers at Oyster Oasis have been raving about the food since its opening last Tuesday – many praising not only the taste but also their appreciation for fresh ingredients from local sources. "I never knew there were so many different ways to prepare oysters," commented customer David Kimberly after trying his first raw, blue crab-stuffed shells with a side of sautéed kelp noodles and wasabi aioli on Wednesday night.

Chef Hayes has emphasized that her restaurant's aim is to provide an educational experience as well – not just in terms of food, but also the impact local seafood businesses can have when they operate sustainably. "I want my customers to know where their meal comes from and feel connected with our community," Hayes says.

Accordingly, Oyster Oasis has partnered with a network of independent fisheries in Charleston for its seafood supply; many members are involved not just as suppliers but also actively participate on conservation efforts to ensure sustainable harvests and habitats. This initiative was put forward after the recent report by The Ocean Alliance, which found that some local shellfish industries had unsustainable practices contributing heavily toward ecological damage in South Carolina's coastal waters.

Oyster Oasis has also taken steps to reduce energy consumption with LED lighting and solar panels on its roof – further reinforcing the restaurant’s commitment towards being environmentally friendly, said Mrs. Hayes confidently stating "It is a responsibility we take seriously."

"It’s wonderful seeing Charleston come together around such a positive initiative," commented Mayor Elizabeth Patterson following Oyster Oasis' grand opening, where she was able to sample some items on offer and even enjoyed the dining experience herself.
