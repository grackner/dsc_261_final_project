Topic: World - India

In a bold initiative to enhance national security and counter terrorism efforts in South Asia, Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi announced Thursday plans for India's first dedicated Anti-Terrorist Center (ATC) located within the strategic depth of Kashmir. The center is set to be operational by December 2025 with a budget allocation exceeding $3 billion and support from multiple Southeast Asian countries, according to official press releases on September 18th in New Delhi at India's National War Memorial event attended by defense minister Rajnath Singh.

Prime Minister Modi expressed that the establishment of this ATC is a response not only to domestic challenges but also as an act of solidarty with allies working towards shared security goals against terrorism on its soil and beyond, which has seen escalating threats over recent years in the region. The decision was taken after intense deliberations among national leaders aimed at fortifying India's defense infrastructure while acknowledging international concerns about regional instability affecting global peacekeeping efforts as reported by security analyst Dr. Priyanka Sharma on September 25th during an interview with the Times of India.

"The creation of this center represents a commitment to ensuring safety and stability within our borders, which is essential for progress," Modi stated at his speech outlining several other initiatives like strengthening cybersecurity measures across Indian states by December 2023 as per the Ministry of Communications' announcement on September 17th. "It symbolizes not just a deterrent to hostile activities but also our readiness to collaborate and share intelligence with partners who have been supportive in these endeavors."

Early reactions from neighboring nations underscore the significance of this development, as highlighted by Singapore's Deputy Prime Minister Teo Han Ghice during his visit on September 20th. "Singapore has always valued strong ties with India; our commitment to peace and security extends beyond borders," he shared at an international summit in Jakarta attended also by officials from countries like Malaysia, the Philippines, Thailand, and Indonesia who voiced their support for this new chapter of regional collaboration.

Indian experts have commended Modi's initiative as a strategic move forwarding India into playing a larger role on international affairs concerning terrorism in Asia Pacific region with respect to security analyst Dr. Sharma, adding that while the ATC will serve immediate needs and actively combat threats, it also sets an example for counter-terrorism measures worldwide: "While today's developments mark a significant stride against potential hazards on India's soil, they simultaneously underscore our capability to lead regional efforts in maintaining peace.
