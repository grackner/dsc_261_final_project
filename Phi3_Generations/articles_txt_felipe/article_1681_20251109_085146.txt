The SEC Extended Bowl Game was set for November 24th at Legion Field in Anniston but will no longer be played due to these challenges, according to Alabama's athletic department announcement on Monday evening.

The decision came after months of uncertainty following the cancellation of many spring sports seasons and impacted events across college campuses nationwide because of COVID-19 outbreaks that hit several universities hard this year in particular. The SEC Extended Bowl Game was intended to give participating teams a chance at extra revenue while helping local economies as well, but with the game now called off and players back home on academic leave or medical withdrawal for safety concerns due to potential exposure from teammates returning early without proper quarantine protocols.

"This decision was not made lightly; it's been a difficult time over these past months trying balance player welfare with the economic needs of our programs and communities," Alabama head coach, Nick Saban said in an interview on Monday evening when asked about why they had chosen to pull out from organizing what would have become one more opportunity for their teams. "But we've gotten feedback directly from parents themselves who are concerned not only over how well prepared all parties were but also worryingly if this was ultimately a decision made because of liability concerns."
