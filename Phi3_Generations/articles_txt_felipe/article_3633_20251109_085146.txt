In a breakthrough for environmental solutions science, researchers at Cambridge University announced on Tuesday that they've developed an affordable and effective water purification device suitable for rural areas in developing countries with access to limited resources. The innovative filtration system boasts the ability to remove contaminants like bacteria, viruses, and chemical pollutants from water sources found both near homes and at remote locations such as riverbanks or ponds within a two-hour cycle using solar power alone without any need for electricity.

Dr Emily Turner, one of the lead scientists on this project alongside her teammates in Cambridge's Department of Civil Engineering said that "This simple technology can bring safe drinking water to many communities across the globe." The device is made primarily with locally available materials such as porous sand and gravel, along with specially designed solar panels which allow it operate efficiently in varying weather conditions without frequent maintenance or calibration needs.
