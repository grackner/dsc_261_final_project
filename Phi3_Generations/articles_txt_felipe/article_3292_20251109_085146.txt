"We have developed an AI that learns and adapts as it operates - this isn't just a step forward; we believe our algorithm will be the industry standard," Thompson stated during her presentation, which was attended by shareholders from across the globe. The company claims its new technology has already been tested in one of their smaller data centers located near Austin, Texas and could bring about 20% savings on energy costs for businesses that implement it into a typical mid-sized server facility with an annual operating cost around $1 million or more to operate.

The algorithm works by continuously analyzing cooling requirements in real time using advanced sensors installed within the data center environment and adapt accordingly, ensuring optimal temperature without overcooling which typically occurs as a result of static rules on how many air-conditioners should run when temperatures rise or fall during operation.
