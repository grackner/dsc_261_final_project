In a stunning turn of events on Tuesday evening in Tokyo, Japan's capital experienced an unexpected power surge that left millions without electricity for hours and disrupted essential services across the metropolis. The National Electrical Safety Committee reported this was the most significant outage since Hokkaido earthquake-related blackouts last year.


Starting at approximately 7 PM local time, residents in central Tokyo began to feel a sudden drop in voltage as transformers struggled under an unusually high load brought on by extreme weather conditions and increased usage of electronic devices during the holiday season. By 9:30 PM, power was fully restored after emergency crews worked tirelessly across multiple districts to manage overloaded circuits before bringing them back online gradually throughout the night.
