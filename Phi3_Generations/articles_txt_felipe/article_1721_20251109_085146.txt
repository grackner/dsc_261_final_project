Topic: Tech - Innovate

A Silicon Valley startup has just unveiled a groundbrethy artificial intelligence system that promises to revolutionize personal finance management for millions of users worldwide without ever touching traditional bank transactions or cryptocurrency markets, reports CNN Business and DailyMail. Introduced this week by InnovateAI in San Francisco on Tuesday, the 'SmartFin' platform uses proprietary algorithms that seamlessly integrate with various financial services to provide personalized investment advice without direct involvement from banks or cryptocurrency exchanges starting April 1st next year.

Founded just last March by tech visionaries Alex Johnson and Maria Chen, InnovateAI aims at making the daunting world of finance easier for everyone with an internet connection to navigate while maintaining utmost security through blockchain-based encryption techniques; their latest release comes after two years of research in partnership with leading banks like JPMorgan Chase & Co. and Wells Fargo, as well as cryptocurrency experts from major exchanges such as Binance and Coinbase to ensure SmartFin's compliance with the best security standards available today.

"We are proud of our latest accomplishment at InnovateAI," Johnson said in a statement shared during Tuesday’s press conference alongside bank representatives and industry experts, "SmartFin is not just another app; it redefines how people manage their finances by leveraging AI with no need for conventional methods of transactions."

"It's about democratizing personal financial management," Chen continued. She explained that SmartFin utilizes a deep learning neural network to evaluate each user’s unique spending patterns, risk tolerance levels and short-term objectives while providing real-time investment recommendations across traditional securities markets as well as emerging cryptocurrencies such as Ethereum (ETH) or Solana (SOL).

Chen highlighted that despite its sophisticated technology, users will not directly interact with SmartFin's trading capabilities; instead the system automates buying and selling orders executed by a distributed network of decentralized financial advisors acting as agents for individual user accounts. "We are taking personal finance to an unprecedented level where everyone gets equal access without fear, bias or intimidation." 

SmartFin's launch also includes partnerships with various local charities and non-profits across the United States allowing users who commit a minimum of $10 per month towards social causes in exchange for investment match rates up to $50 over time – this initiative was inspired by Johnson’s personal experience as an immigrant entrepreneur.

As SmartFin gains popularity, concerns regarding the safety and security remain paramount among skeptics questioning how such a platform could potentially access sensitive user data; however InnovateAI executives assured all communication with their system is encrypted via blockchain technology ensuring absolute confidentiality from prying eyes.

"We’ve been rigorously testing SmartFin since its inception and have implemented top-tier cybersecurity protocols to prevent any form of data breach," Chen said during Tuesday's press conference, adding "we are transparent about our operations but will maintain discretion on individual user matters."
