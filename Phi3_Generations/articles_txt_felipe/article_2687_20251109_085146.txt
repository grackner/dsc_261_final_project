Topic: Sports - College Football

Washington Redskins Alumni Led Historic Win for Old Dominion University at Fiesta Bowl on New Year's Eve Night; NCAA Division I College Football Pennant Race Brings Closure to Season with Standout Performance in Semifinal Game Against Ohio State Buckeyes, Official said.

Monday’senjoyed an incredible display of talent and unity as the Washington Redskins alumni played a crucial role in leading Old Dominion University's victory at Fiesta Bowl on New Year's Eve Night against the undefeated Ohio State Buckeyes, according to NCAA Division I college football officials.

"It was an absolute masterclass," said Steve Spurrier Jr., former Washington Redskins head coach and current Old Dominion University athletic director who witnessed his alumni's game-winning performance firsthand on Sunday night at the stadium in Albuquerque, New Mexico. "They came together as a team and demonstrated remarkable skill."

This thrilling win marked one of only three instances where an NCAA Division I college football championship game was played after Christmas since 1956 when major bowl games became popular to celebrate the holiday season's most important sporting event. Both Fiesta Bowl in Albuquerque and Rose Bowl in Pasadena were hosting semi-finalists with Alabama Crimson Tide, Texas A&M Aggies from College Station, among other finalist teams including Oklahoma Sooners (Oklahoma City), Oregon Ducks (Portland) NC State Wolfpack & Wake Forest Demon Deacons headlining this year's Fiesta Bowl while USC Trojans were participating in the Rose Bowl game.

The Redskins alumni, who once donned that iconic uniform during their college days at The George Washington University around 40 miles from Old Dominion University; embarked on an epic journey to secure a spot among Fiesta Bowl finalists under Spurrier's watchful eye.

"The team spirit and unity displayed by these players remind me of my time with the Redskins," said Jim Bostick, former GWU player who now works as assistant coach for Old Dominion University women’s basketball program in Arlington County near Richmond since 2016. "It's amazing to see them achieve this kind of success after graduating."

Old Dominion played Ohio State Buckeyes, led by third-year quarterback Justin Fields on Saturday before defeating Clemson Tigers with the help of senior running back Zac Brown Jr., a three-time first team All-American. This victory propelled them into semifinals where they faced Alabama's highly decorated Heisman Trophy winner, Derrick Henry in Fiesta Bowl as per NCAA Division I college football schedule that usually concludes on New Year’s Eve Night when champions crowned by fans celebrating the holiday season.

Despite not being a traditional rivalry between GWU and ODU's past teams during their respective eras, Spurrier noted how strong connections were formed among these former classmates who now share common goals in pursuit of greatness on field: "This wasn't just an alumni gathering but genuine team building. They showed that even though they are not playing every Sunday together anymore."
