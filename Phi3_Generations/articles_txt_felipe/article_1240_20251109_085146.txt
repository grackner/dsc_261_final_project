Topic: World - China

Amidst concerns over escalating tensions in the South China Sea, a group of foreign journalists arrived yesterday to investigate allegations that China has been engaging in aggressive territorial expansion at sea under pretense of building artificial islands and reef structures within international waters. The inquiry by an independent panel of reportons from across Asia was launched on Tuesday after the United Nations released documents indicating increased military activity near disputed maritime zones, prompting urgent calls for transparency.

The foreign journalists began their investigations at Pearl Harbor in Hawaii as a first step toward understanding China's global naval presence and reach overseas without causing any diplomatic flare-ups with the Chinese government; however, tensions rose after several interviews were conducted along the coastal regions of Guangdong province.

"We heard from multiple sources that there is significant construction activity taking place in areas close to disputed territories," said James McKeon, a senior military analyst and expert on East Asian affairs at New York University's Stern School of Business who has followed the territorial dispute closely for decades but had never seen anything like this. "It appears that China is not only expanding its physical presence in these areas via land reclamation projects to claim sovereignty, they are also constructing airstrips and facilities capable of supporting military aircraft operations."

One official from the Chinese Embassy told CNN International: "China's peaceful development and activities on this front have never been misinterpreted as expansionism. We believe in resolving disputes through dialogue, not force or coercion," adding that Beijing was committed to maintaining regional stability while working constructively with other claimant nations like the Philippines, Vietnam, Malaysia, Brunei and Indonesia over these territorial waters where crucial sea routes for international trade pass.

As Chinese officials denied any illegal activities were taking place at Pearl Harbor or along their coastline during a press conference held yesterday in Manila with Philippine Foreign Secretary Teodoro Locsin Jr., more than two dozen journalists and photographers have been granted access to the disputed waters off southern China, where U.S.-funded research vessels are currently conducting seabed surveys along Chinese-claimed territories within 20 nautical miles of Hainan Island under a recent memorandum signed between USPACOM (U.S. Pacific Command) and the Philippines military last year on mutual defense against piracy or terrorist threats in Southeast Asia, as well as possible freedom-of-navigation operations around disputed maritime zones that would be observed by some of their Chinese counterparts under a separate agreement between Beijing's Peopleâ€™s Liberation Army and the PLA Southern Theater Command.
