Topic: World - Europe

A significant earthquake struck northern Italy on Saturday evening, causing a spate of structural damages and trapping multiple people in partially collapsed buildings across Turin and its surrounding areas. At around 7:45 PM local time (18:45 UTC), the magnitude-6.2 tremor originated near Langhe mountains west of Turin according to preliminary data from Italy's national earthquake monitoring agency, Istituto Nazionale di Geofisica e Vulcanologia (INGV).

As witnesses described it: "I felt a strong shaking in my kitchen and then heard the sound like something breaking. I quickly ran outside." Marianne Bottazzi shared this account with local TV broadcries after calling emergency services to report multiple injuries at her workplace near downtown Turin where power lines sparked, causing further chaos on already devastated streets.

Emergency responders are working around the clock amidst reports of collapsed buildings and numerous roadblocks due to damage; many victims still trapped in partially destroyed structures await rescue efforts by specially trained urban search-and-rescue teams from across Italy, as well as international aid workers arriving today.

Mayor Gianluca Carli told a press conference earlier that day: "The situation is dire but hopeful - there are people still inside these buildings and we need their cooperation to get them out safely." With thousands of residents displaced from the earthquake, local schools have been converted into temporary shelters for those in urgent need.

Residents anxiously awaited news on when they might be able to return home as many faced emotional and material losses due not only within their own properties but also among cherished family heirlooms that couldn't withstand the tremors. In response, Italian Prime Minister Giorgia Meloni emphasized national unity: "Our first priority is helping our people recover from this tragedy with the government providing immediate relief support." The UN Refugee Agency echoed a similar sentiment stressing that Italy has historically been generous in times of crisis and remains committed to assisting its earthquake-hit neighbors.

Efforts are currently focused on securing all unstable structures while continuously searching for survivors, with the Italian military providing security at makeshift camps near some heavily damaged sites where residents have sought temporary refuge as they wait to hear about potential rebuilding efforts and when their homes might be safe again.
