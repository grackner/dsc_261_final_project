In a stunning development on Tuesday evening in Chicago, authorities were alerted to an emergency as four teenagers found themselves stranded and unresponsive near the Millennian Park. Emergency services responded at about 8:30 PM when bystanders discovered them lying motionless approximately half a mile from safety along Lake Michigan's shore, according to officials with the Chicago Fire Department (CFD).

"We got a call for help around this time yesterday evening regarding four young individuals who were reportedly stranded on one of the city beaches," said Captain James Smith, head of public relations at CFD. "They initially survived exposure but then succumbed to hypothermia when their clothes became soaked and they failed to find shelter."

The teenagers, aged 16-20, were last seen enjoying the sunset on Wednesday afternoon near one of Chicago's popular boardwalk attractions. They had been caught in an unexpected rain shower that persisted for hours before lifting up late into Thursday night as they attempted to return home via public transportation without success due to disruptions caused by severe weather warnings issued across the Midwest region, reports CBS News.

"I felt helpless as I watched them shivering against the cold," recounted Maria Gonzalez, a regular jogger along Lake Michigan's promenade who noticed their plight upon first seeing out-of-breath and disoriented teens wandering aimlessly on one of many sidewalks leading down to waterfront properties during her early evening walk.
