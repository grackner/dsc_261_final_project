Topic: Politics - Elections

As midterm elections approach on Tuesday in the United States with high voter turnout expected nationwide and particularly significant races set to take place across Florida's 27 congressional districts, political analysts are watching closely as both parties ramp up campaigning. This year marks an especially critical time for American democracy given that Congress will hold a session at the Capitol in Washington D.CTY immediately after voting is done this evening to certify election results on November 14th by state legislatures across all states with midterm elections taking place, including Texas's highly contested races where Republican candidates are gaining traction ahead of Election Day which was delayed from last Tuesday due to a hurricane that hit the Sunshine State hard.

"With Florida in mind right now for me it seems like every vote counts just as much, if not more than others," stated Emily Thompson-Smith who lives near Miami Beach and has followed politics since she was young but never voted until this year due to her disillusionment with politicians over the past few years.

Florida will see seven seats in its House delegation up for reelection, including a race between incumbent U.S Representative Ted Deutch and challenger Nicole Maloney from Miami-Dade County who both vied hard during primaries last month with voter turnout already exceeding 50% nationwide as of Monday according to The New York Times which reported that Democrats had about a three percentage point lead over Republicans in Florida based on exit polls.

"I think this election is going more towards giving power back for control," said Michael Sanchez, political science professor at the University Of South Florida St Petersburg and host of Political Pulse Radio Show & Podcast who has been covering politics closely since he first started teaching his now-over 20 years ago. "We can expect some surprises on Tuesday night as we never know what will happen until after casting your ballot."

Biden administration officials are warning that potential turnout could change if the federal government is shut down once again which has been a point of contention between Democrats and Republicans for months now. The two parties have struggled to come to an agreement on infrastructure spending leading many Republican senators like Senator Ted Cruz from Texas who spoke at rallies overseas last weekend, claiming that his party is being unfairly targeted by the media as they try negotiate better terms for their state.

"I don't think anybody should underestimate either side of this election because we still have a few surprises left in store with some seats decided on Tuesday night," said Sanchez referring to several races across various states where voters will make choices between candidates like Uvalde Republican Tom Rooney or New Jersey Democrat Mikie Sherrill who challenged her incumbent colleague Congresswoman Carolyn Edwards from District 8 which is considered one of the most competitive seats nationwide.

As election day approaches, political parties continue to push their agendas and policy proposals through social media platforms while also organizing rallies in local communities across America aimed at mobilizing support among voters before casting ballots on November 3rd which has been set as the new Election Day following last year's historic elections that resulted in Congress gaining more Republican members amidst controversy over election security and fraud allegations.
