BREAKING NEWS: Unprecedented Snowstorm Hits New England This Weekend; Officials Urge Public to Exercde Extreme Caution as Avalanche Risk Increases  
By Sarah Thompson, Meteorologist and Associate Reporter for CNN - Tuesday, March 14, 2023   
NEW YORK — A powerful blizzard dubbed the "Snowstorm Sentinel" has descended upon New England overnight. As of this morning in Boston, Massachusetts, at least two inches of snow have already blanketed city streets and suburbs since early Saturday evening. The National Weather Service warns that heavy precipitation is expected to continue throughout the day today with a significant risk for dangerous ice accumulation on roadways across multiple states—including Connecticut, New Hampshire, Vermont, and Maine.  

In light of these forecasts, Boston Mayor Jane Wallace has announced an emergency snow response plan aimed at ensuring citizen safety as well as maintaining transportation services during the storm’s duration in this densely populated region. "We are taking every possible precaution to keep our citizens safe and comfortable while they ride through these record-breaking conditions," Wallace said today, speaking from City Hall amidst piles of snow cleared just hours ago.  

While Massachusetts Gov Phil Hartman urged residents in a press conference last night not only take shelter indoors but also check on elderly neighbors or those living alone as they navigate the hazardous roads and icy sidewalks created by this unprecedented weather event, local officials are preparing for an emergency response if necessary.  

According to Dr. Richard Foster, a renowned climatologist at UMass Amherst who has been studying winter storm patterns in New England since the 1980s and is now working with state meteorologists on risk assessments: "It's important for everyone affected by this severe weather event to understand that such intense cold snaps are not entirely unexpected given our regional climate. This snowstorm may have been one of the harshest ever recorded."  

As temperatures continue to plummet below freezing and wind speeds pick up, meteorologists warn that ice accumulations could pose serious safety risks on roadways while also making travel nearly impossible across all major routes in Massachusetts as well as parts of New Hampshire. In addition, the heavy snowfall has brought down power lines resulting in thousands of local households throughout Maine and southern Vermont experiencing temporary blackouts due to storm-induced outages at utility companies struggling with an already overwhelmed infrastructure during this season's brutal cold snap that began last weekend. 

Despite efforts from multiple agencies including the Red Cross who have sent hundreds of volunteers and relief workers across several states, residents are still urged to stay indoors until temperatures rise above freezing again for at least three consecutive days as forecasted by experts on Wednesday morning. With over 30 inches expected in some areas this weekend alone before tapering off into a mix of sleet and rain tomorrow afternoon, authorities continue their preparation against snow-related disasters while providing support services to those most vulnerable amidst the region's wintery siege.
