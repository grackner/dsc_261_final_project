The damage was sustained when he tried to make an ambitious sidestep on third down in overtime with 30 seconds remaining—a play that would have potentially sent his team soaring into a national championship game had it succeeded. Instead, the Hawks' hopes of breaking their five-year postseason drought dashed under cloudy skies as fans watched helplessly from bleachers and stands while Turner was carried off on a stretsuit with a grimace etched across his face.

This turnover comes after UT swept through their regular season with an undefeated record while boasting a top-ten national ranking, but now it seems the Crimson Tide have been dealt an unfortunate hand on what could be seen as one of college football’s biggest stages this year.

Turner's father and former NFL quarterback Jamal Turner spoke to reporters at his son’s press conference: "It was a tough pill for my son to swallow, but I know he won’t let it define him or hold back against anyone out there."
