CNN/DEV EXCLUSIVE: Major Uptick in Office-Based Workout Programs Emerge as Pandemic Normalizes  
September 25th, by Sarah Miller and James Anderson - New York Times Bureau   
In the shadow of unprecedented public health challenges that have come with COVID-19 pandemic lockdown orders, a surprising trend has emerged within office walls. A significant increase in organized fitness programs tailored for professionals working remotely or those restricted to their workplace is being observed across major cities like New York and San Francisco.

This evolution of the corporate wellness industry came into focus when multinboned technology firm, TechStellar Inc., launched its virtual gym initiative aimed at keeping employees active during long hours behind screens—a move that quickly caught on within other Silicon Valley-based companies as they seek to support workforce health and morale.

"We're seeing a real transformation in the way we approach physical fitness with our staff," said Alex Thompson, Director of Employee Wellbeing at TechStellar Inc., "Our program not only addresses their mental well-being but also ensures they are physically active despite being away from traditional office environments."

The initiative is an ambitious project consisting of personalized workout routines and live streaming with professional trainers. Participation data shows a whopping 70% surge in employees engaging twice daily since the program's launch last month, as per internal surveys by TechStellar Inc., suggesting that workers are making space for physical fitness within their new normals—a sentiment echoed across Wall Street to Silicon Alley.

Similar programs have sprung up spontaneously with companies like Quantum Dynamics and BrightFuture Analytics offering a suite of virtual classes from yoga mats in home offices to high-intensity interval training (HIIT) sessions for those looking at their health holistically, reports the New York Times. A representative from HealthWise Fitness says that these programs are crucial during times when gyms have yet to fully reopen and public parks still carry a risk of virus transmission: "The corporate world has stepped up in remarkable ways—bringing fitness directly into people's homes where they work, it shows an understanding we all need now more than ever."

In major urban centers such as Los Angeles County Office workers have started to form 'virtual boot camps,' with over 150 sign-ups for each session held weekly. The LA Chamber of Commerce reports that these programs are helping maintain workplace productivity and morale, while the city's leading health experts applaud such innovative approaches: "It’s a positive step towards incorporating fitness into daily routines which is vital in mitigating stress," says Dr. Elizabeth Carter, an epidemiologist at Los Angeles General Hospital.

Experts believe this trend has roots deeper than just corporate wellbeing strategies—many people have reported finding balance and camaraderie through these programs that they sorely lacked while working remotely or within office spaces limited by social distancing measures: "I've reconnected with old colleagues during our weekly yoga class, it feels like a part of the team again," shared Michael Brown, an employee at BrightFuture Analytics who has been taking their daily cycling challenge.
