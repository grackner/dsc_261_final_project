In a daring heist that has rattled communities across three states, an alleged group of five purportedly claimed responsibility for the brazen robbery at Secure Bank in Atlanta on Thursday evening (June 15). The suspects reportedly managed to bypass high-tech security systems before making off with $2.3 million worth of cash and jewelry, according to preliminary police reports released Monday morning as investigations are still underway nationwide.

Bank officials have issued statements expressing concern over the brazen robbery that took place in Atlanta's secure bank facility on Thursday evening (June 15), leaving an estimated $2.3 million worth of cash and jewelries missing, with initial investigations continuing nationwide as a group is said to have claimed responsibility for the heist via social media platforms earlier this weekend.

"I was here at the bank when they arrived," says witness Sarah Jennings from her home in Decatur about 10 miles away just after midnight on Thursday, June 15th "They cut through backdoors and somehow managed to get past all our security cameras without setting off any alarms. It was like nothing I've ever seen before."

Bank officials say that the robbery happened at about 9:30 p enda night when three robbers entered wearing high-tech masks disguising their faces while four others created chaos inside and outside, leading to a tense situation throughout Atlanta. The suspect is still on the run after escaping police in broad daylight from secure bank facility situated near Grant Park at about 10:27am local time following approximately an hour-long chase involving multiple patrol cars.

Local authorities describe it as one of their most challenging robberies to date and insist that the suspects were well-coordinated in executing a plan they claim was inspired by criminal activities portrayed on streaming television shows, though no connection with any specific programs has been established so far according to law enforcement officials.

A local resident of downtown Atlanta explains how such incidents are affecting his neighborhood: "People used to feel safe coming and going at night - now everyone is walking around looking over their shoulders like it's Halloween or something." The sense of fear has left a lasting impression on the community that may take months if not longer to shake off, according to social workers working in local neighborhood clinics.

The FBI and Interpol have both been alerted about this multi-state crime which appears linked with previous heists across Georgia including one reportedly committed at New York Bank last month where $10 million was allegedly stolen using similar methods used by the Atlanta group; no arrests were made as of now.

Detective John Matthews, who is leading investigations into these brazen robberies says his team has received tips and witness statements which could lead to potential suspects within a week or two but insists they would continue their relentless pursuit until the perpetrators are brought to justice. "The motives behind this crime remain unclear as of now, although we do believe these individuals had planned for months prior," he said during an interview today (June 21).
