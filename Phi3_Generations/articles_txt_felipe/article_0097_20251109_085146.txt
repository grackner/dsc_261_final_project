A groundbreaking initiative is set to transform Thai cuisine in Bangkok into a healthier alternative while preserving its cultural essence as local chefs band together for this culinary revolution beginning next month, reports CNN. Chef Ananya Suriyasarn, known locally and internationally for her sustainable approach to cooking, is at the forefront of spearheading a movement aimed towards incorporating more plant-based dishes in traditional Thai restaurants without compromising on authenticity or taste. The chefs believe this shift will improve public health outcomes while honoring local customs and flavors inherent to Bangkok's food scene.

"I truly feel that with the right combination of vegetables, herbs, spices and proteins, we can create dishes full of vibrant colors and intense flavors without using any animal products," says Ananya as she prepares a batch of 'Pad Thai' for tasting in one of Bangkok's most popular restaurants.

Chefs have been experimenting with different ingredients to develop an array of dishes that are both vegan and authentically representative of the richness found within traditional cuisine, says chef Somchai Prapasitkit who has led workshops on sustainable cooking for chefs across various establishments.

"The goal is not to replace a classic dish but to evolve it by substituting meat with plant-based proteins like soy or seitan," adds Somchai, emphasizing the importance of understanding traditional recipes as foundations before adapting them into healthier versions. 

This initiative comes at an opportune time when fast food chains have been increasingly criticized for their impact on public health and obesity rates across urban centers globally; including Bangkok which has seen a sharp rise in such concerns, reports DailyMail.
