Topic: Health - Relationships

EUROPEAN HEALTH CARE SYSTEMS AND RELATIONSHIPS

By Alex Taylor

June 15, 2023 - European Union health authorities are set to introduce new guidelines aimed at addressing the relationship between mental well-being and intimate relationships. Experts suggest that better understanding of these dynamics could lead to improved public policies across member states by mid-2024.

In a first for Europe, studies indicate more than 35% of adults have experienced some form of anxiety or depression linked with their partner's issues at least once in the past year—a significant increase compared to previous data collected before COVID-19 upended normal social patterns worldwide. These mental health challenges are reportedly causing strain and conflict within many relationships across all socioeconomic backgrounds, according to a comprehensive survey released last month by EU's European Union Agency for the Quality of Medicines (EUAQM).

Dr. Helena Rodriguez, Director-General at EuroCare Institute in Madrid said during an interview on June 10 that "our interconnected world means no nation can exist without robust relationships—physically and emotionally." She added: “This new initiative focuses not only on individual therapy but also how partners support each other's mental health.”

A central aspect of this multi-national effort, set to launch at the European Health Summit in Brussels tomorrow where delegates from over 40 countries are expected, involves workshops and training programs for couples aimed at strengthening communication skills about sensitive topics. The EU's Commissioner for Social Affairs has pledged additional funding of €5 million to this project as part of their commitment towards a healthier European society by fostering strong interpersonal relationships that can cope with the challenges posed today’s world, including pandemics or economic crises.

A 34-year-old Dublin teacher who participated in pilot programs during her free time commented on how she and her husband learned to listen more empathetically without judgment when discussing personal issues at home - “It's changed our relationship dramatically, we both feel closer than ever”, said Eileen Murphy.

EUAQM chief scientist Dr. Thomas Lister highlighted the importance of these findings in a recent interview on BBC Radio 4: "We often hear about mental health as an individual issue; however our studies clearly demonstrate how interconnected personal and relationship well-being are - more than ever before, particularly post-pandemic."

The initiative is set to expand over the next two years with plans including collaboration between psychologists in member states, couple's counselors offering support across borders within EU countries as part of cross-cultural exchange programs aimed at building a more united and healthier Europe. The hope now lies on policymakers heeding these findings to create tailored national strategies by next year that could reshape the face of European society for generations to come, ensuring robust relationships form an integral part of our holistic approach towards public health in this modern age.
