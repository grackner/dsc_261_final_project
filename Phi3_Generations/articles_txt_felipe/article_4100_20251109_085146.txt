Topic: Politics - CNN Polls

Amidst a year of intense political campaigning and fiery rhetdess across America, new data has emerged from an ongoing series by CNN that underscores the divisions within American society today more than ever before. The latest survey conducted in mid-October reveals significant shifts in public opinion since last month's results were released—particularly along racial and economic lines.

In a comprehensive poll involving nearly 10,000 Americans from all corners of the nation with varying degrees of political engagement, CNN researchers found that respondents continue to express deep-seated concerns about race relations in America's capital city—Washington D.C. The study shows an increasing sense of distrust among citizens regarding government efforts aimed at bridging racial divides despite substantial investments and initiatives launched by the current administration.

"There is a palpable frustration within our communities that have historically borne the brunt of systemic injustices," says Dr. Maria Lopez, sociologist with expertise on race relations at Howard University. "People are looking for more than just empty promises from those who hold power."

The poll indicates a stark contrast between different demographic groups' perceptions of progress towards racial equality; while young adults and minority respondents largely believe there has been little to no change, older citizens feel some improvements have taken place. Despite recent events highlighting police brutality against African Americans being fresh in public memory due to the death of George Floyd this spring, CNN found that only 48% of those surveyed expressed confidence that actions are being taken by authorities within their respective communities; meanwhile, just over half (51%) believe little or nothing is happening.

"For many Americans today—especially young people and minority groups living in urban areas like DC—the reality on the ground feels far removed from rhetoric about unity," says Prof. James Smith of Columbia University's Politics Department who was not directly involved with CNN researchers but has studied similar issues for years now. "Our democracy depends upon a solid foundation where all citizens feel heard, represented and safe."

Experts say that the findings reflect growing societal concerns about race relations in America as well as how effective politicians are at communicating changes within their governing policies—or lack thereof—to constituents who continue to experience hardship. While many agree on needing progress toward a more inclusive society, some experts question whether the current administration's strategies hold enough weight or substance behind them that they can bring forth long-lasting change among citizens struggling with racism in America for decades now.
