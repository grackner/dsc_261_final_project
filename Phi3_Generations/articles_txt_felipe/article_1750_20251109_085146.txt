A recent outbreak of a tropical virus has prompted authorities to impose strict travel restrictions between Miami and Central America as they attempt to contain its spread. The Department of Health announced the measures on Thursday morning with immediate effect, citing concerns about rising infection rates among residents and visitors alike near coastal resort towns like Cabo San Lucas, Tijuana, and Panama City.

Miami-Dade County Mayor Carlos Gimenez said during a press conference that "the health of our community must come first" as officials seek to limit the virus's impact on tourism revenue in one of Florida’s most popular destinations for international visitors from Mexico, Canada, and South America.

“We are taking this step out of an abundance of caution,” said Dr. Ashley Bailey-Kingson, Director of Infectious Diseases at the University Hospital Miami Shores. “With over 400 confirmed cases in our community within just two weeks, it’s clear that action is needed to protect public health.”

Residents who were returning from international trips reported feeling alarmed about what they perceive as overly stringent measures being taken.
