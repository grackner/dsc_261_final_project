Topic: Style - Luxury

A Paris-based luxury fashion house announced plans to launch a groundbreding initiative designed to bridge economic gaps within local communities while promoting its exclusive brand image worldwide. The high street boutique 'Elle Délice' will inaugurate the program, aimed at empowering artisans from underserved neighborhoods in Lyon and Marseille by integrating their craftsmanship into ready-to-wear collections for Spring/Summer 2024.

Founded almost two decades ago on Rue Saint-Nicaise's fashion district, 'Elle Délice', recognized as a beacon of luxury style in France, is taking significant steps toward social responsibility and inclusivity by giving rise to an unparalleled collaboration with local artisans. The initiative seeks not just to uplift the economic prospects for these craftspeople but also maintain high-quality standards expected from 'Elle Délice'.

"The essence of our style is deeply rooted in traditional French techniques, and by collaborating directly with talented artisans on the ground level - Lyon and Marseille - we hope to blend this rich culture into our upcoming collections," declared Pierre Dubois, co-founder of 'Elle Délice'.

The initiative was born from a desire not just for economic upliftment but also cultural preservation. It came as an answer when the fashion industry faced criticism over environmental and social concerns in its supply chains worldwide – including fast food franchises, technology companies with massive data centers consuming enormous power resources or multinational corporations heavily criticized due to labor laws abroad.

Sitting alongside a burgeoning artisan community that has long been ignored for the production of luxury goods in France was an opportunity too compelling not to explore further, say 'Elle Délice' founders - they believed there is room on high fashion runways and living rooms worldwide without diluting their signature chic style.

"We aim at producing a range that strikes the perfect balance between our sophisticated Parisian flair with French craftsmanship while simultaneously enhancing opportunities for local artisans," stated Marie Moreau, Elle Délice's Head of Sustainable Development and Inclusivity.

To achieve this objective successfully, 'Elle Délice’ has partnered with two non-governmental organizations - ‘Les Artisans du Coeur Lyon', an association founded in 2018 to support traditional craftspeople within the city of Lyon's historic textile sector and similarly operating organization ‘Cœur Marseille'. These partnerships will help 'Elle Délice' gain insights into local artisan skills while also providing guidance on quality control, brand alignment and marketing.

As per Marie Moreau, "The goal is to provide these talented individuals with a chance not just for economic upliftment but ensuring their unique creations become an integral part of our fashion lineup without compromising the exclusivity that 'Elle Délice' stands for."

Artisans selected through rigorous screening processes will be integrated into Elle Délice’s Parisian studio, gain exposure to luxury markets and access essential training on brand standards.
