"We've never seen anything quite this bad before," said Chief Fire Officer Michael Anderson from the Miami-Dade County fire department who was among hundreds of emergency personnel working tirelessly through Friday night to rescue trapped individuals amidst submerged cars, homes, bridges, and roadways. "The extent of damage is absolutely heartbreaking."

This historic weather event has left at least 20 counties under states of disaster declaration with more than three million residents impacted directly or indirectly by the storm's aftermath so far today (March 16). The National Guard was mobilized across these hardest-hit areas to assist overwhelmed local authorities, while a growing list of national and international aid organizations have pledged support.

A representative from FEMA stated on Sunday that they are closely monitoring the situation in Florida as well its neighboring states which also experienced heavy rains and flooding; however he added there would be no immediate federal disaster declaration at this time due to insufficient deaths or damages reported so far: "We're prepared for anything," said Representative John Carter.
