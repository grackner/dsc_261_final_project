Johnson adds that her children had mentioned seeing a van with tinted windows parked outside the back entrance during their break time on Thursday evening and early Saturday morning before they were due home from school around 9:30am—"the whole situation is just so unnerving," Johnson stated.

Police initially responded to reports of odd behavior at Fletcher High School after hours last week, but the true motive for the unusual activity remains unclear as investigations continue this morning with no additional arrests made yet since Friday night's events when four suspects were cornered by officers near Lombard following a tip-off call.

"Our team continues to work tirelessly on these leads, and we urge anyone who might have information regarding the robbery or suspicious individuals around this school district to come forward without hesitation," said Captain Michael Rodriguez with CPD's Special Operations unit in a Friday night statement.

The Fletcher High School cafeteria was closed for breakfast as part of regular cleaning, and students were instead brought into their classrooms where they remained until parents picked them up at 9:30am on Saturday when the unexpected commotion began; several reports suggest that a brief scuffle outside school grounds had ensued prior to police being notified.

According to CPD Lieutenant Sarah Martinez, who was first assigned to investigate suspicious behavior after Fletcher High School closed at 7:30pm last Thursday evening following an uncharacteristic visit by unknown men dressed in dark clothing—possibly security staff for the property or individuals with criminal intentions.

"When I arrived on scene, there were only two vehicles present which looked like maintenance trucks; something didn't seem quite right about these activities," Lieutenant Martinez said Friday as she continued her investigation overnight and again today at 6:00am before the alarm was triggered inside Fletcher High School prompting immediate lockdown procedures.

School authorities were notified of unusual activity outside school grounds shortly after, but they believed nothing suspicious happened due to miscommunication with police communication lines; it wasn't until parents noticed their children had returned late and in shock that questions arose about what exactly took place at the cafeteria on Thursday evening.
