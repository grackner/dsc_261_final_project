Topic: Tech - Innovate

A group of local teenagers has been praised for developing an innovative gardening technology that increases crop yields without increasing resources or pesticide use in rural Kentucky on April 15th. The team, consisting of four high school students from the small community of Pine Ridge, presented their creation to agricultural experts and received a standing ovation during last week's science fair at Ashland High School.

Dubbed "AgriTech," this gardening tool uses sensor-based monitoring systems that precisely deliver water, nutrients, and light exposure based on each plant'selive needs to optimize growth rates while significantly reducing the environmental impact of traditional farming methods in areas with limited resources like Pine Ridge.

Abby Thompson, 17 years old from Ashland High School shared her insights into their project - "We wanted something that could help our community and provide food security without harming Mother Nature." Her teammates Samantha Jackson, Emily White, and Jordan Roberts echoed the same sentiments while discussing how they envision AgriTech helping farmers in other parts of rural America.

Farms across Kentucky have already begun trialing this eco-friendly technology after hearing about its success at Ashland High School's science fair last month, with many expressing optimism for the future it could bring to struggling communities like Pine Ridge that face economic and environmental challenges alike.
