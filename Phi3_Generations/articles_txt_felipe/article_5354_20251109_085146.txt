A peaceful protest broke out in Kyiv this Sunday as citizens took to the streets demanding political reforms and accountability for human rights abuses during a period of unrest since last year' extramarital affair scandal involving politicians rocked Ukrainian politics, CNN reported. The demonstrations were initially sparked by shock over an allegedly leaked conversation between prominent figures in Ukraine that has been widely circulating on social media channels and prompting accusations against them for corruption within the government apparatus.

Overnight Friday into Saturday at 10:30 p.m Kyiv time, thousands of people joined hands to march together as part of a coordinated movement across various cities in Ukraine calling for transparency from their political leaders and an end to widespread corruption within the country's governmental institutions since President Petro Poroshenko took office on May 25, just before Russia invaded. The allegations stemming from this leaked audio have only fanned already-burning public resentment among Ukrainians against their political elite who many believe are taking advantage of the country's misfortunes for personal gain.

"For too long we've tolerated corruption and injustice, but it is time to demand change," said Maria Pavlyuk, a 45-year-old school teacher from Lviv participating in one such protest march near Maidan Nezalezhnosti Square - the epicenter of all major Ukrainian political unrest since Russian tanks rolled into Kyiv on February 27.

In Kyiv alone, according to activists' estimates reported by BBC News Ukrainian Reforms Monitor (URM), approximately 20,000 people participated in the marches on Sunday amidst some clashing with police who have been deployed for over a month patrolling city streets as part of security measures against potential further escalations following Poroshenko's contentious cabinet reshuffle last weekend. A few hundred individuals were detained after being accused by law enforcement officials in Kyiv and elsewhere to be carrying banners with explicit messages, while local NGO Transparency International released a report warning that the current political climate could further erode public trust if not addressed immediately due to fears over corruption.

"It's imperative for those holding power nowadays take concrete actions against wrongdoings and start restoring faith among Ukrainians," said Oleg Zhyttiaiev, chairman of Transparency International Ukraine chapter who has witnessed firsthand protest movements since 2014 Euromaidan movement when Poroshenko came to office as the new President. "The leaked audio shows just how far things have come down in terms of accountability within our political system."
