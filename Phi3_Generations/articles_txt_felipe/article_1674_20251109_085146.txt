Topic: Science - Climate

A major breakthrough in climate research has been made by scientists at NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory as they report unprecedented ice loss from Greenland and Antarctica over the past decade. The findings, based on satellite data, reveal a significant acceleration of melting rates compared to previous years.

Scientists have been closely monitoring glaciers in both regions for decades now but what has come as an eye-opener is how quickly this meltwater could potentially raise global sea levels by several meters over the next century if left unchecked, say experts from Columbia University's Earth System Science division.

"This study underscores just how urgent it becomes for us all to take action in order prevent runaway climate change," says Dr. Sarah Johnson, lead author of a new report on glacial meltwater impact published earlier this month and an associate professor at the university's School of Arts & Sciences based out of New York City, adding that "we are rapidly approaching tipping points which might push us into irreversible changes."
