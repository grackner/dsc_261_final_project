Topic: Politics - CNN Polls

A major new poll suggests that public confidence in President Biden'in handling of foreign relations has seen a notable increase since he took office last year amidst growing global tensions with China and Russia. Conducted by the Pew Research Center, this comprehensive study shows American opinions have evolved following policy shifts under Biden’s administration towards Asia-Pacific nations, traditionally viewed as areas of concern in U.S.-China relations due to disputes over trade practices and human rights issues with Hong Kong's protesters.

The Pew Research Center report found that 63% now express trust in Biden’s handling compared to just 48% prior, a change attributed largely to his administration pursuing more cooperative policies on the world stage since taking office last year and re-establishing relations with allies after years of strain under former President Trump.

Dr. Alice Martinson, lead researcher for Pew said: “We found that trust levels have risen across all demographics which suggests a widespse shift in public opinion about foreign policy.” 

“It seems the American people are finally seeing results from Biden's early efforts,” added Paul Williams of Washington University. He highlighted increased U.S.-Japan diplomatic talks and trade agreements as pivotal moments sparking positive change, alongside successful negotiation with North Korea which has seen them agree to halt testing nuclear weapons without any major repercussions from the international community thus far.

However critics point out that these findings do not necessarily reflect a true appraisal of foreign policy successes but rather increased optimism as Biden moves towards domestic agendas such as healthcare and climate change, which they argue should be prioritized over global tensions where progress has been slow at best.

"Bringing our troops home from Afghanistan while still facing an existential threat elsewhere is a dangerous balance," commented former diplomat James Bennett who now works with Human Rights Watch advocating for human rights around the globe despite his criticism towards Biden's foreign policies thus far arguing that "the world doesn’t stand to benefit when nations prioritize their own domestic issues over global responsibilities."

Regardless of differing opinions, it appears Americans are more hopeful about U.S.-foreign relations now than in the immediate aftermath of former President Trump's term — but whether this positivity will translate into tangible improvements remains to be seen as Biden continues his journey forward amid international affairs where progress can often feel incremental and frustratingly slow for those eager for change."
