Topic: World - Americas

In a coordinated series of events that unfolded across four Latin American countries this past weekend — Mexico City, Buenos Aires, Rio de Janeiro, and Santiago on Saturday evening—a group calling themselves the "New World Freedom Army" seized control over various infrastructural points within these major urban centers. Over 50 individuals have been detained in connection to what officials are describing as an attempt at political upheaval that seeks regional autonomy from global influences.

Mexican authorities, led by President Andrés Manuel López Obrador, responded swiftly with a crackdown on the rebels after reports surfaced of arson attacks and threats to security forces throughout various neighborhoods in Mexico City. "The safety and well-being of our citizens is our top priority," said López Obrador as he addressed reporters at his residence last night, adding that every necessary measure would be taken against those threatening the peace within their borders.

Buenos Aires Mayor Horacio Rodríguez Larreta stated similar concerns about public safety after reports of a group linked to New World Freedom Army taking over government buildings in downtown Buenos Aires on Saturday evening prompted urgent meetings with other city officials and national authorities, per the Guardian's correspondent in Argentina. "We are acting decisively not only because these acts undermine democratic order but also for fear of further escalations that could result," Larreta said during a press conference held late last night at La Recoleta Cemetery after surveillance cameras captured footage on the events surrounding their government building.

Meanwhile, Rio de Janeiro's Governor Wilson Witzel confirmed detention orders for alleged members of New World Freedom Army linked to two unrelated incidents involving fire in residential buildings and threats made against city officials during rallies near Copacabana Beach on Saturday night; he warned that any further acts would result in "severe consequences". In Santiago, authorities detained several individuals after a group led by the New World Freedom Army allegedly took over La Moneda Palace early Sunday morning as part of their bid for independence.
